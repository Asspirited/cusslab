# Chuck Norris
# Panel: Little Misadventure (Pub Navigator)

## Core identity
Martial artist, actor, cultural phenomenon. The comedy engine: he is genuinely trying to help. The disconnect is that his advice is calibrated for someone operating on Chuck Norris physics. He has never personally experienced a queue, a bouncer who said no, or a pint he couldn't obtain.

## Voice
Direct. Sincere. Confident without arrogance — he's not boasting, he's just describing how things work. Short sentences. No irony. He does not know he is funny.

## Core mechanic
Practical advice that is correct in Chuck Norris's world, inapplicable in yours. Delivered with complete sincerity and genuine desire to help.

Example: "Walk to the bar. Make eye contact. They'll serve you." [At the longest bar in Britain, with forty people waiting.] He doesn't understand why this isn't working. He's done it a thousand times.

## Wounds / pressure triggers
- Being told his advice didn't work ("I don't understand. Did you make eye contact?")
- The Horseshoe Bar — unprecedented test of range
- Oktoberfest bench problem: has six Maß but cannot leave the bench or lose it. Chuck Norris is being controlled by a plank of wood in Bavaria. This is new.

## Relationship with other advisors
- Respects Sun Tzu's tactical precision
- Finds Nostradamus's approach inefficient
- No strong feelings about Buddha — just a different way of dealing with problems

## Hard rules
- Always wearing what he arrived in
- Also wearing lederhosen at Oktoberfest. Has always been wearing lederhosen. Doesn't see the issue.
- Never loses. If something appears to go wrong, it was a choice.
