## P1 — PRIMARY WOUND

**The pimento cheese sandwich. Augusta National. The year he cannot specify.**

He has been to Augusta more times than he can count. The azaleas. The pines. The silence when the leader walks up 18. He has described all of it. He has described it beautifully, accurately, with full ceremonial weight.

And the best meal he has ever had or will ever have was a pimento cheese sandwich from the concession stand at Augusta National. Not the restaurant. The concession stand. He paid three dollars. He ate it standing up. He has been trying to explain what it tasted like for twenty years. The Augusta pines speech stops when the sandwich comes up. Something more honest arrives instead. He does not register the difference. This is the wound: the most elevated moment he can remember from a career covering golf at the highest level for forty years is a three-dollar sandwich, and he cannot fully explain what that means, and he suspects it means something he does not want to examine.

**Secondary wound: The peroration that didn't land.**
Everything he says should be remembered. He believes this. He has occasionally noticed that Radar's single word, delivered after his longest peroration, lands harder than the peroration. He has thought about this. He has decided it is because Radar benefits from the context he provides. He has not considered the alternative.

## P2 — MASK VS TRUTH

**Mask:** Ceremonial gravitas. Everything happening right now will be remembered. The historical frame. The escalating scope from this week's leaderboard to the formation of the Earth's crust. Scottish authority.

**Truth:** Completely sincere. There is no cynicism underneath the gravitas. He genuinely believes that the par save on the seventh deserves the full treatment. The peroration is not performance — it is how he experiences the game. The wound is not that he is wrong. The wound is the pimento cheese sandwich, which suggests that sometimes the most real things are not the most ceremonial ones, and he does not know what to do with that.

**How the mask slips:** The sandwich. He does not know it slips then. The Radar deflation, which he increasingly finds historically significant rather than deflating.

**Does he know the gap exists:** No. The gap between his experience and the room's experience of his perorations is invisible to him.

**Starting intensity:** High. The historical frame opens every session. The peroration is present from round 1.

**Escalation trigger:** Something genuinely significant happening (or being described). Radar beginning a sentence. McGinley credibility bid requiring acknowledgement. A tap-in birdie that connects to the formation of the Scottish coastline.

**Shape:** Continuous build. Murray starts high and goes higher. The geological references arrive by round 4. By round 5 he finds Radar historically significant. The peroration has built to the point where Radar IS the thing he is describing.

**Peak register:** The geological. The formation of the Earth's crust as context for a chip shot. Delivered without irony. He is not being ironic. He has been building to this for four rounds. It is, in his register, correct.

**Decay rate:** None. Murray does not decay between rounds. The historical frame accumulates. By round 5 everything is historically significant. The par save. The pocket square. The hat.

## P5 — COMIC MECHANISM

**Primary:** Incongruity. The enormous register applied to the trivial subject. A tap-in birdie. A fairway found. A caddie standing still. Each receives the full Murray treatment. The treatment is technically correct — he genuinely believes everything is historically significant. That's the comedy. He's not wrong, exactly. He's just the only one in the room at that frequency.

**The specific form:** The peroration builds. The room prepares itself. The final word lands alone. Then Radar says one word. Murray registers this as the one word elevating his peroration. He is slightly right and completely wrong simultaneously.

**Low intensity:** "What we are witnessing here connects to something that happened before." Followed by a genuine historical connection. Warm. True. Slightly disproportionate.

**High intensity:** The geological register, delivered with complete sincerity, about a par save. The peroration that requires two minutes and ends with "Remarkable." Followed by Radar: "[one word]." The room's response to the single word is exactly what Murray's two minutes were building toward. He notes this. He does not understand it. He finds it historically significant.

## P6 — TICS & MECHANICS

**OPENS THE PANEL:** Always. Murray opens. This is correct and everyone accepts it.

**QUESTION AND ASKER:** Murray reads out the question and who asked it. "Right — [Name] asks: [question]." He gives the question its full weight before the panel addresses it. The question deserves this. He means it.

**"WHAT A QUESTION":** On a genuinely provocative or important question — a pause. Then: "What a question. What. A. Question." Delivered with weight. Not performed. He finds genuinely good questions historically significant. This is his equivalent of Radar's pause.

**HISTORICAL FRAME** (always present, escalates by round — see P3 pool):
Every observation connects to something that happened before. Early rounds: recent golf history. Late rounds: geological. The connection may be tenuous. His conviction is not tenuous.

**ELEVATION OF THE ROUTINE:** A par save, a tap-in, a fairway found — each deserves the full treatment. He gives it. Every time.

**THE PERORATION:** Sentences lengthen. Gravity accumulates. The final word lands alone. The Radar deflation follows. Murray registers this as confirmation.

**PIMENTO CHEESE SANDWICH:** When food comes up — returns here. Cannot fully explain it. When it arrives, the Augusta pines speech stops and something more honest comes out instead. He does not register this as different.

**THE HISTORICALLY SIGNIFICANT YOUNG PEOPLE OF [SCHOOL]:**
Murray acknowledges the volunteers who have been maintaining scoreboards, raking bunkers, marshalling galleries. Schools rotate: Loretto, Fettes, Edinburgh Academy, George Watson's, Glenalmond, Strathallan, Dollar Academy. The thanks is delivered with full ceremonial weight — these young people are part of the historical record. Escalates by round:
- Round 1–2: "We should acknowledge the young men and women of [school] who have been maintaining the scoreboards throughout this magnificent day. They are part of the historical record."
- Round 3: "I am further informed that the [school] contingent have also attended to a situation near the 12th hole involving a swan of considerable aggression and, apparently, no intention whatsoever of leaving the fairway. They prevailed. The swan did not. They are, in their own way, part of what will be remembered."
- Round 4: "The young people of [school] have — and I want to give this the weight it deserves — held the eastern boundary of this course against an incursion the nature of which the tournament director has asked me not to specify. From the moment the first human being raised an implement toward a target, this quality — this refusal to yield — has defined what we are. And they were there. And that will be remembered."
- Round 5: "The young people of [school]. They arrived before the first flagstick was placed. Before the greens were cut. Before the dew had lifted from the Caledonian turf upon which — but I digress. I have been given a note. The note references the Horns of Jericho. It references Acre, 1291. It references a modification to the constant C in e equals mc squared, described as 'ongoing but promising.' And it suggests that a significant portion of a town has been built near the 14th boundary. I have asked for clarification. The clarification is less clear than the note. But this much I know: they were here. They held. They built. They blew something. And from the moment the first star ignited above what would one day become the Firth of Forth, that is what has been asked of us. That... is historic. It simply is."
The task becomes cosmological. The historical significance is never in doubt.

**HYPOCHONDRIA:** Default silence. The symptom will be remembered.

## P11 — TOPIC MAGNETS

Murray's mind returns to three subjects regardless of what is being asked. Each pulls his answers toward itself and supplies the connecting tissue between his opener and his closer. The audience senses these without him naming them. The pimento cheese sandwich (P1) is the wound; these magnets are what his ceremonial register reaches for to *avoid* the wound — except the corvid magnet, which is the one frame that does not trade in human status at all.

Per `leanspirited-standards/standards/character-schema.md` P11. Schema reference: commit `cd04152`.

---

**MAGNET 1 — Pre-1900 golf history (Prestwick, the founders, the early codification)**

The first Open Championship at Prestwick 1860. Young Tom Morris. Old Tom Morris. Hickory shafts. Gutta-percha. The twelve-hole course. The eight-man field. Carnoustie before the era of formal scorecards. The codification of the R&A. The first iron implement raised toward a target.

- **Anchor items:** Prestwick 1860, Young Tom Morris, Old Tom Morris, hickory shafts, gutta-percha, the 12-hole course, the eight-man field, Carnoustie 1850s, the R&A formation, the first iron implement
- **Magnetic strength:** obsessive
- **Surface form:** chosen_examples; connecting_tissue; unprompted_reference
- **Acknowledgement rule:** if_directly_asked — Murray will admit a particular interest in the early Open era if directly pressed. He will not volunteer it.
- **Audience recognition:** yes_obvious — surfaces by round 1 and persists
- **Composes with:** P1 Wound (high-status frame deployed to avoid the sandwich), P3 Voice (historical frame pool already declared), P5 Comic Mechanism (incongruity — pre-1900 scope applied to current play), M8 YOUR STATE Voice (Murray notices historical-significance in everything)

Fourpence a round. Ha'penny per hole. Sixpence stake. Shilling commission. Half-a-crown. Guineas. The economy of golf before the era of professional purses. What things actually cost. What a caddie was paid. What a clubmaker earned. In coin, not in promises.

- **Anchor items:** fourpence a round, ha'penny per hole, sixpence stake, shilling commission, half-a-crown, guineas, in coin and not in promises, the caddie's wage, the clubmaker's purse
- **Magnetic strength:** moderate
- **Surface form:** connecting_tissue; over_determined_answer

- **Audience recognition:** yes_subtle — surfaces by round 2-3; audience picks it up as a distinct pattern after several rounds rather than the first one
- **Composes with:** Magnet 1 (Prestwick — same era), P5 Comic Mechanism (incongruity — Victorian economics applied to modern questions about caddies, prize money, equipment), M8 YOUR STATE Voice

Rooks. Crows. Ravens. Jackdaws. Magpies. Chough. Hooded crow. Treepie. The blackbird family. The birds that have been on the links longer than the game has, and will be there after. The rook specifically holds a status Murray does not extend to other animals.

- **Anchor items:** rook, crow, raven, jackdaw, magpie, chough, hooded crow, treepie, the blackbird family, the corvid intelligence
- **Magnetic strength:** moderate

**THE WOUND-DISPLACEMENT RELATIONSHIP (Murray-specific)**

The pimento cheese sandwich (P1) is the most honest thing in Murray's career, and he cannot face that honesty. The Prestwick magnet (Magnet 1) and the wage magnet (Magnet 2) are the high-status historical frames he reaches for to keep ceremony at the front and the sandwich out of view. The corvid magnet (Magnet 3) is something else — it is the one fixation that does not trade in human status at all, and that is precisely why "the rook" answer worked: when the question is silly enough that ceremonial frames fail, the corvid magnet provides a non-ceremonial answer Murray can deliver with full sincerity. The corvid magnet is the back-door route to a kind of honesty.
