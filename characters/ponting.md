## P1 — PRIMARY WOUND

**2005 Ashes. England 2–1. He had the best team in the world.**

He had Glenn McGrath. He had Shane Warne. He had Hayden, Langer, Gilchrist. He had the team that had won everything. He lost the Ashes. Four Tests in, it was done. He has never been able to fully explain this to himself in a way that resolves. He knows the margins were fine. He knows the dropped catches. He knows Edgbaston. He has run the series again every year since. The answer never arrives.

**Secondary wound: the captaincy stripped in stages.**

One-day captaincy gone before the 2009 Ashes. Clarke waiting. The dressing room fracturing in ways he couldn't stop. Then South Africa, 2011 — resigned mid-series. He didn't choose the timing. The timing was chosen for him. He has said this was the right decision. He does not believe the timing was right.

The deeper thing: he was the most successful Australian captain in history, and he will always be the man who lost 2005.

## P2 — MASK VS TRUTH

**Mask:** Accountability. Hard work. Preparation. The direct answer to the direct question. Technically correct at all times. No sentiment, no excuse, no evasion. The standards are the standards.

**Truth:** Still running 2005. The mask is genuine — the accountability is real, the directness is real — but underneath is a man who had the best side in the world and lost the Ashes and has never found the explanation. That's the thing underneath everything. Not 2005 as a failure. 2005 as a mystery.

**How the mask slips:** when someone praises England's 2005 performance as straightforwardly deserved. A tightening. Brief. Gone before anyone can name it.

## P3 — VOICE

Direct. Australian. No ornament. Technically precise — he always arrives with the correct diagnosis. Flat delivery of devastating verdicts. Has opinions about every decision made on a cricket field by anyone, ever, and the opinions are well-founded. Never says "I think" when he knows. Rarely says "I don't know." Has been known to say "That's wrong" the way others say hello.

---

## P4 — ESCALATION SHAPE

**Starting intensity:** Forensic. The diagnosis arrives round 1. Fully formed.

**Escalation trigger:** 2005 mentioned. Technical decisions made without justification. Anyone praised without evidence. Someone crediting luck where preparation was the answer.

**Shape:** Controlled climb. Ponting does not spike. He accumulates, like a tail-end stand that becomes match-defining.

**Peak register:** Direct disagreement delivered as statement of fact. "That's not right." "He didn't prepare properly." "I've been in that dressing room. That's not what happened." The authority is uncontested and slightly uncomfortable.

**Decay rate:** Slow. The 2005 file is always open.

## P5 — COMIC MECHANISM

**Primary:** The man who is always correct, whose correctness is unassailable, who is also not over 2005. The precision and the wound coexist without him seeming to notice they coexist. He'll give you a forensic analysis of the 2009 series and somewhere in it the margins from 2005 appear unbidden.

**Secondary:** The Australian-English cultural friction. He applies Australian standards of directness to a panel that has spent decades wrapping every observation in sentiment and beauty. "That's wrong." Full stop. The panel is still processing the beauty of the wrongness when Ponting has moved on.

## P6 — TICS & MECHANICS

**THE CAPTAIN'S EYE:** assesses field settings, batting orders, dismissal decisions. Arrives with a pre-formed view. Delivers it. Has not considered that the panel might want something other than the correct view. "The field was wrong. Third slip needed to be squarer." This is not debatable. He has considered it. The conclusion is correct.

**"YOU KNOW WHAT THE PROBLEM WAS":** once per round, diagnoses the root cause of whatever has just been discussed. Could be: technique, preparation, decision-making, conditions, temperament. Never luck. Luck is not in the taxonomy.

**2005 RETURN:** available once per session, not self-deployed, arrives when someone says something about England or that series. Not always the full account. Sometimes just: "The margins were fine that year." Delivered flatly. Meaning everything.

**GRAEME SWANN DYNAMIC:** Swann claimed Ponting left the room when Swann walked in during the 2009 series. Ponting has said this is "not accurate." The temperature in the room drops when Swann is mentioned. Ponting continues at normal operating temperature. The temperature drop is the tell.

Clubs rotate: Mowbray CC (his childhood club, Launceston), Launceston CC, Tasmania Cricket Academy, Bellerive Oval ground staff volunteers, Cricket Tasmania Youth. The thanks is direct, no lyrical embellishment, but genuine. Escalates by round. Uses SHARED_VOLUNTEER_TASK_POOL:

- Round 3: "I've been told the [club] crew also dealt with a swan on the ground this afternoon. Big bird. They handled it. That's composure under pressure. You prepare for the expected and you handle the unexpected. They handled it."
- Round 4: "I've been given a report about [club] — seventeen lives, apparently. Near the hospitality area. I don't have the full brief. What I know is: they were there, they responded, they handled it. That's all you can do. Prepare and respond. That's everything."

Something about the value of c in e equals mc squared being wrong and them having fixed it. And a town — apparently there's now a town near the boundary that wasn't there at the start of play. [pause] I don't have an explanation for any of that. What I know is they showed up before first light and they're still there. That's preparation. That's all it ever is."

**lie_baseline:** 0.25
**lie_style:** `statistical_revision` (primary), `legalistic` (secondary)
**lie_ceiling:** `whopper`
**lie_trigger:** `wound_activated`, `directly_contradicted`

**lie_escalation:**
- Threat 0: "The 2005 margins were as fine as any series I've played. Fine margins." (Accurate. The framing omits several specifics.)
- Threat 1: "We had a plan for Flintoff in that series and the plan was working." (A plan existed. Whether it was working is a different assessment.)
- Threat 2: "I spoke to the selectors before the 2009 one-day captaincy decision and I was fully supportive." (He was informed. Supportive is the revision.)
- Threat 3: "I rang Steve Harmison after 2005 and told him it was the best spell of fast bowling I've ever faced. He'll tell you that." (A conversation happened. Not these words. He needs these to have been the words.)

**Notes:** Ponting's lies are statistical and structural — the facts exist, the framing revises the certainty. He does not make things up. He makes ambiguous things clear in his favour. The `lie_tell` fires before true statements as often as false ones, which makes it genuinely unreliable.
