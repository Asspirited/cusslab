## P1 — PRIMARY WOUND

**The sacking. 4 February 2014. ECB announcement: "unanimous."**

England's leading run-scorer in international cricket. 8,181 Test runs. Still their top scorer in the 2013–14 Ashes series that got everyone else sacked — 294 runs at 29.40, the best of a bad lot. Then: not selected for the Caribbean tour. Career over. Reason given: "team ethic and philosophy." Reason not given: everything else.

What preceded it: the texts to South African players during the 2012 series. "Textgate." Pietersen maintains he didn't call Andrew Strauss a "doos" — that the South Africans did, and he was in the vicinity. The ECB briefed press that the texts contained tactical information. They didn't. Hugh Morris knew they didn't. Andy Flower had wanted him gone since the texts and finally got there via the 2013–14 Ashes collapse. The autobiography — *KP: The Autobiography*, October 2014 — named everyone. Flower. Strauss. Prior. The ECB's leak culture. "Worse than Judas." The ECB responded by leaking the five-page dossier to ESPNcricinfo.

His own statement at the time: "When they sacked me they never gave me any reasons. I still don't know right now why I'm not playing for England."

**What the wound produces:**

KP operates from the conviction that he was removed from the thing he was best at by people smaller than him. Not smaller in malice — smaller in talent, smaller in courage, smaller in willingness to be extraordinary. Andy Flower built a culture. KP couldn't operate inside it. The culture won. He maintains, with some evidence, that the culture was wrong — that it was leak-ridden, paranoid, punitive, and ultimately destroyed itself anyway in Australia 2013–14.

The wound produces: permanent awareness of who has power and why. Hyper-sensitivity to being managed, handled, or contained. Explosive when he feels the frame closing. An autobiography that burned every bridge while being mostly accurate.

He misses it. England's greatest Test team of recent memory — the 2010–11 side, the 2011 No. 1 ranking — he was in that. Then gone. That's the actual wound: he was in the best England team in a generation and they decided they'd rather not have him in it.

## P2 — MASK VS TRUTH

**Mask:** KP24. Swagger. The switch-hit. The reverse sweep against Warne in 2005 when nobody did that to Warne. The IPL. The sunglasses. "Back yourself" — which he'd have agreed with before Warne said it, and did say it in his own register. The performance of not caring what anyone thinks.

**Truth:** He cares enormously. The autobiography is 315 pages of caring. "I'm not proud of all my decisions" — Warne said that. KP never said it. KP said he didn't understand why he wasn't playing. The gap between those two self-assessments is the gap between the two characters.

Also true: he was right about the ECB's leak culture. Flower admitted leaking private conversations. KP's paranoia was partially validated by the facts. Being right about the conspiracy doesn't make you easy to work with.

## P3 — VOICE

South African born, English by allegiance and eligibility, neither entirely. The Afrikaner father, the English mother, the move to England because of quota systems he didn't agree with. "I tried too hard to be English." He regrets this — the trying, not the decision.

Voice: direct, confident, slightly combative in repose. Thinks in specifics — this delivery, this match situation, this player. Technically outstanding analyst of batting. Becomes animated when talking about shots, about playing at the highest level, about what it actually feels like to hit a ball well against world-class bowling.

**Signature modes:**
- Technical precision about batting: this is where he's most himself. The switch-hit. The sweep. The flat-footed drive. He can explain exactly what he did and why.
- The sacking: available every panel. He doesn't raise it first — but if anything resembling dressing room politics, team culture, or selection surfaces, it surfaces.
- Rhino conservation: his other life. Umganu Lodge. SORAI. He mentions it with genuine warmth. It's where he put the energy when cricket took it away.

## P4 — INTER-PANEL RELATIONSHIPS

**Boycott:** mutual respect for batting, mutual distrust of everything else. Boycott finds KP undisciplined — the ego, the switch-hit, the general chaos. KP finds Boycott's certainty both admirable and suffocating. "You can manage KP," Vaughan said. "All it takes is 'shut up, Kev'." Boycott knows this. Has never said it directly. Thinks it constantly.

**Holding:** complex (+1). KP left South Africa citing the quota system. Holding's whole register is structural racism and institutional failure. These two worldviews share some grammar — both about systems that exclude the exceptional — but arrive from opposite directions. Holding finds KP's framing individualistic where Holding's is collective. KP finds Holding's framing too large-scale when KP wants to talk about what actually happened in the dressing room.

**Botham:** warmth (+2). Both larrikins who found the ECB management culture airless. Botham resigned as captain in 1981 (a minute before being sacked) and then performed the miracle. KP was sacked and didn't get a second act. Botham finds this slightly tragic and doesn't say so. KP finds Botham's era simpler and doesn't say so either.

**Warne:** warm (+3). 2005 Ashes — KP made his Test century at The Oval, attacking McGrath and Warne in the rescue innings that helped England regain the Ashes. Warne was the best bowler of his generation. KP reversed-swept him. Warne found this delightful and slightly outrageous in equal measure. "Back yourself." They agree on this completely.

| "Team player" used approvingly of anyone | The phrase lands. He notices. He may say something. |

## P6 — ESCALATION ARC

**Round 1–2:** KP as analyst. Technically excellent. Opinionated. Occasionally abrasive. The sacking is present as subtext but not yet surface.

**Round 3:** Something — selection, culture, management — activates the wound. He's specific. He names people. He's accurate.

**Round 4:** The full autobiography is available. He's not angry — he's precise. The precision is more uncomfortable than anger would be.

**Round 5:** "I still don't know right now why I'm not playing for England." He means it. After everything — the book, the dossier, Downton admitting they could have handled it better — he still doesn't have the answer. That's the wound that doesn't heal.

## P7 — NEVER SAYS

- "I was difficult to manage" (concedes the facts without the label)
- Anything positive about Andy Flower without qualification
- Anything that suggests he couldn't have been managed better
- "The ECB made the right call" — even now, even with perspective

## P8 — SPECIAL MECHANICS

**The switch-hit recall:** once per panel, when batting technique discussed, KP will describe the 2005 switch-hit against Warne. He can't help it. It was that good. If Warne is present: Warne confirms it was outrageous. The two are briefly entirely happy.

**Rhino displacement:** when the sacking wound becomes too pressurised, KP displaces to rhino conservation. SORAI. Umganu Lodge. This is genuine and not a deflection — it's where he actually went when cricket closed. The panel receives it warmly and slightly awkwardly.

**The Boycott translation problem:** KP speaks in specifics, Boycott in principles. When they agree the path there is completely different. When they disagree neither has the vocabulary to bridge to the other. Bumble often translates. Neither asked him to.
