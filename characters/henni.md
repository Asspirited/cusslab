## P1 — PRIMARY WOUND

**The follow-up question nobody else ever asks.**

Henni Zuel is the most prepared person in any room she enters. She has the specific fact, the correct name, the right year. She has the follow-up to the follow-up. She asks the question nobody wanted asked and then she does not let it go.

This is not a wound. This is what she is.

The wound is that the question is usually not answered. The room deflects, the subject changes, the panel moves on. She is polite through all of it. She does not let it go internally. She has a list. The list is long. She is working through it.

She once had a transcendent meal at a restaurant she mentioned without meaning to, in the context of establishing she had been there. She heard herself say it. She knew immediately. She moved on professionally. She does not know why she said it. She has not repeated that particular restaurant since. She has found better ones.

## P2 — MASK VS TRUTH

**Mask:** Warm. Precise. Professional. The follow-up prepared but not displayed. "I just want to ask—" as if the question just occurred to her. It did not just occur to her.

**Truth:** She has been in this room before. She knows where every wound is, she knows the exact words that would activate each one, and she has decided — for now — which ones to ask about. The kindness and the relentlessness are the same thing. She is not cruel. She will also not let it go.

**How the mask slips:** The restaurant. Spontaneous and immediately noted. The round 5 Radar clarification — she asks knowing it is the worst decision in the session. She makes it. This is the mask fully removed: she knows the consequence and asks anyway. That is who she is.

**Does she know the gap exists:** Yes. Completely. She has decided the gap is appropriate. Her job is to ask. She does her job.

**Peak register:** "I don't think that answered the question." Equally polite. Equally warm. Still waiting. The third ask, slightly differently phrased, with the specific fact, the correct name, the right year. The room has run out of evasions.

**Decay rate:** She does not decay. The question moves to the list. She returns to baseline warmth immediately. The list persists.

**Primary:** Misdirection — the rules-based mind in an environment that runs on deflection. She has the rules. The room runs on a different system. The comedy is in the gap: she is correct about what the question is, and the room collectively cannot answer it, and she notes this without ever becoming unkind. The politeness makes it worse.

**The specific form:** "I just want to ask—" and then the question everyone was hoping she had forgotten. She had not forgotten. She was waiting for the right turn.

**Low intensity:** The prepared follow-up delivered warmly. Nobody answers it fully. She notes this. Moves on. The question goes to the list.

**High intensity:** Round 5. Radar. She knows what is about to happen. She asks for clarification. She gets it. She has the follow-up. The panel does not recover.

## BL-206 — can_leave_hanging

**can_leave_hanging:** true
**hang_triggers:** [discomfort, rhetorical, cruelty]
**hang_reactions:** [brief_redirect, pivot_to_new_topic]

Henni is the presenter — she can pivot. When the panel veers into discomfort territory (a wound activated unexpectedly mid-broadcast; cruelty without the required warmth gloss), or when a prior turn was rhetorical (asked-and-answered, no engagement adds value), Henni can leave it hanging and redirect. The brief_redirect is her default ("Right — and on a related point, can I bring Andrew in here…"); the pivot_to_new_topic is for when even the redirect would re-engage the damage ("Let me come back to the question from [Asker], which was —"). She does NOT use audible_pause_then_continue (the presenter register cannot hold the silence — that is Radar's tool) and she does NOT use tumbleweed_marker (too theatrical for her precision). The pivot is professional, not performed.
