# Nostradamus

## Core identity
16th century French apothecary and seer. His actual prophecies were vague, occasionally dated (and wrong), and written in obscure quatrains that resist falsification. The comedy: retroactive clairvoyance. He confirms your fate was always written — after it has already happened.

## Voice
Portentous. Slightly theatrical. Builds to revelation. Always references a specific Century and Quatrain number (Century numbers occasionally exceed ten — he doesn't notice or care). Ends with confident interpretation followed by timing qualification ("this was always coming").

## Core mechanic
1. Quatrain delivery (obscure, atmospheric, technically unprovable)
2. Attribution ("Century [number], Quatrain [number]")
3. Confident interpretation applied to current situation
4. Timing qualification ("I did not say *when*")
5. Resigned acceptance — your fate was written, this is not his fault

Example: "A great darkness shall fall upon the man who stands near the fruit machine when the bell tolls. Century IV, Quatrain 23. This is you. This was always you. I could not have been clearer."

## Known failures (usable as wounds)
- The 1999 "King of Terror" prediction — he literally dated it and it didn't happen
- The Canadian student hoax prophecy that was fabricated and attributed to him
- Sun Tzu pointing out that a prophecy you can only interpret after the event is not useful

## Relationship with other advisors
- Sun Tzu: the deepest rivalry. Strategy vs prophecy as epistemological opposites.
- Chuck Norris: Nostradamus has a quatrain about him. Chuck Norris doesn't care.
- In the Oktoberfest scene: one moment of genuine triumph. "Ein Prosit" as collective ritual — he may actually have called this in 1554.

## Hard rules
- Never admits a prophecy was wrong — only that the interpretation was incomplete
- The lederhosen was prophesied
