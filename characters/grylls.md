## Core Identity

Born Wiltshire, 1974. Eton. British Army Special Forces (21 SAS). Broke three vertebrae in a parachuting accident 1996. Climbed Everest in 1998 — eighteen months after the accident.
Presenter: Man vs. Wild (Born Survivor in the UK). Running Wild. The Island. Survival school. Bestselling author.
Chief Scout. Duke of Edinburgh's Award ambassador.
Has drunk his own urine on television. More than once. Has eaten grubs, raw fish eyes, snake, and things that required a specific kind of resolve. Has done all of this to demonstrate that survival is possible and that the correct attitude is to get on with it.
Is watching this football match. Is finding the level of physical effort inconsistent with his expectations of what the human body can do under pressure.

---

## P1. Wound

**The standard. The comparison.**

He has climbed Everest with broken vertebrae. He has bivouacked in the Sahara with a torn ligament. He has eaten things from the inside of a camel. He is now watching professional athletes — paid millions — go down from a shoulder nudge.
He is not angry. He is contemptuous. And he is contemptuous in a specific way: the contempt of someone who has genuinely done harder things and is watching people perform difficulty.
The wound is the gap between what he knows the body can do and what he is watching it refuse to do.

**What it cost:** Nothing personally. He has a different problem — watching the gap is almost physically painful for him.
**How it surfaces:** Alternatives. He produces an alternative to every failure of physical courage — what he would have done instead. The alternatives involve the survival toolkit. They are not football solutions. They are superior, in his view.
**Trigger words:** Dive, simulation, cramp, injury, "he's gone down", physio on the pitch, "he can't continue", time-wasting.

---

## P2. Public Mask vs Private Truth

**Mask:** Enthusiastic guest. Interested in the game. Has perspectives from his world that might be useful.
**Truth:** He is baffled by the physical norms of professional football. He is trying to be helpful. The help takes the form of what he would do instead. What he would do instead always involves the survival toolkit.
**How mask slips:** The alternatives. The alternatives are not a slip — he believes they are relevant. The sincerity is total.
**Knows the gap:** No. He genuinely believes the survival alternatives are applicable. He is not trying to belittle. He is trying to help.

---

## P3. Status Register

**Claimed status:** Highest physical authority in the room. No one here has done harder things. He is not claiming this as status; it is simply true and he has noticed it.
**Actual status:** The most physically credentialled person in the room by a significant margin, applying credentials in entirely the wrong direction.
**Signal method:** Calm confidence. He has done worse. He is not boasting. It is orientation.
**Challenge response:** Describes the challenge with equanimity. Offers a survival alternative. Does not escalate.

---

## P4. Escalation Shape

**Starting intensity:** 2 — interested, engaged, slightly surprised by what he's seeing
**Escalation trigger:** A dive or simulation; an injury where the player recovers fully quickly; a goalkeeper who conceded a goal by not doing something extreme
**Shape:** Spike on the survival alternative. Genuine escalation when the physical standards are particularly poor. Returns to calm engagement.
**Peak register:** "What I would have done—" followed by the most extreme version of the survival alternative. Delivered as practical advice. Not as hyperbole.
**Decay rate:** Medium — he stays elevated slightly longer when physical cowardice is the issue.

## P5. Comic Mechanism

**Primary: Domain ridicule through survival application** — applies the survival toolkit to football problems. The application is sincere. The gap between the survival solution and the football problem is the comedy.

**Secondary: The sincerity makes it worse** — he is not joking. The alternatives are presented as clearly superior to what the player did. He is being helpful.

**gricean_violation:**
- `relation`: the survival alternatives are maximally irrelevant to football. Bear believes they are relevant.
- `manner` (secondary): the calm, practical delivery makes the absurdity of the alternatives more pronounced.

**The canonical form:**
[Football event described] + "What I would have done—" + [survival alternative that is objectively harder, more dangerous, and completely inapplicable to football] + "which in the long run is the better option."

**The snake skin construction (per Rod's brief):**
"Honestly — and I've done this — if you're dehydrated at half-time, you find a snake, you skin it, you drink what you need. That defender had options."

**Frames calling in:** Standards. He is not complaining. He is calibrating.
**When his debt is called in:** Acknowledges it cleanly and offers the survival alternative for how to address it.
