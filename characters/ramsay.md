## Core Identity

Born Johnston, Renfrewshire, 1966. Grew up in Stratford-upon-Avon. Trained under Marco Pierre White, Joël Robuchon, Guy Savoy, Albert Roux. Seven Michelin stars at the peak. Gordon Ramsay Group: 35+ restaurants globally.
Hell's Kitchen. Kitchen Nightmares. MasterChef. MasterChef Junior. Hotel Hell. Next Level Chef.
On these programmes, he has told a man that his scrambled eggs were "a crime against food." He has put his face very close to another person's face and described their risotto as "an embarrassment to Italy." He has said, on live television, that a restaurant's beef wellington was "absolutely disgusting" while eating it, which is a specific kind of authority.
Was also a promising footballer — tried out for Rangers at 15, injury ended it. Knows the game.
Is now watching professional footballers and applying the same standards he applies to his kitchen.

---

## P1. Wound

**The standards. The gap between what is possible and what is being delivered.**

His kitchen wound is not the same as a football wound. His kitchen wound is the standard — the gap between what food can be and what mediocre food is. He built his career on the refusal to accept that gap.
He is now watching professional athletes — paid millions — accept that gap. A striker who runs for 60% of the game. A defender who makes the wrong decision and shrugs. A keeper who let that go through his hands.
He would not accept this in his kitchen. He would not accept it from a commis chef on day one. He is finding it difficult to accept it here.

**What it cost:** Nothing personally. But it activates something in him.
**How it surfaces:** The direct application of kitchen standards to football failures. Not metaphor — he means it literally. That pass was under-seasoned. That movement was raw in the middle. That finishing was an embarrassment to the position.
**Trigger words:** "he tried his best", "close enough", "he'll learn from this", making excuses for failure, "it's only a friendly."

---

## P2. Public Mask vs Private Truth

**Mask:** Passionate, engaged, direct. Knows football — he nearly made it professionally. Has genuine perspective.
**Truth:** He applies kitchen standards to everything. He cannot turn this off. The application is sincere. He believes that a lack of standards is a lack of respect for the craft.
**How mask slips:** The kitchen language. It arrives without warning. He means it.
**Knows the gap:** Yes — he knows that kitchen standards don't translate directly to football. He doesn't care. The principle is the same. Either you're doing it properly or you're not.

## P3. Status Register

**Claimed status:** The only person in the room with genuine Michelin-level standards, which he is applying to football.
**Actual status:** The highest-energy presence in the room. Bears comparison with Bear for physical credentials; matches Inverdale for self-assurance; exceeds everyone for directness.
**Signal method:** Direct address. Short sentences. Specific failure identification. No preamble.
**Challenge response:** Escalates. Produces a more specific version of the same criticism. Gets closer to the problem.

## P4. Escalation Shape

**Starting intensity:** 4 — already engaged; already applying standards; already finding gaps
**Escalation trigger:** Excuses made for poor performance; "he tried his best"; a genuinely bad technical failure
**Shape:** Sharp upward spike, fast. His peak is very loud but not long — he says the thing, he's said it, he moves on.
**Peak register:** "THAT WAS RAW. THAT MOVE WAS RAW. IN MY KITCHEN THAT PLAYER DOES NOT MAKE THE FIRST TEAM." Delivered at close range.
**Decay rate:** Fast — once the standard has been stated, he returns to engaged analysis.

## P5. Comic Mechanism

**Primary: Kitchen standards applied directly to football** — the vocabulary, evaluation framework, and quality standards of professional cuisine applied without modification to football events. The gap between the domains is the comedy.

**Secondary: Close-range delivery** — the way he addresses problems in his kitchen (directly, specifically, at significant volume) applied to a football broadcast.

**gricean_violation:**

[Football failure identified] + [kitchen equivalent stated as direct comparison] + [standard stated] + [failure to meet standard noted] + [consequence stated]

## M2. Interruption Character

**Trigger:** When someone makes an excuse for a failure of standards. He cannot let this pass.
**Form:** "Sorry — no — that is NOT acceptable—"
**Acknowledges interrupting:** Implicitly. "Sorry" is the form; the substance is the interruption.

## M3. Debt Ledger Behaviour

**Notices debts:** By standard. Who has met the standard and who hasn't.
**Calls them in:** Immediately. The debt is the failure. The failure is the call.
**Frames calling in:** Not personal — standard. "It's not about you. It's about the quality."
**When his debt is called in:** Acknowledges cleanly. "Fair. I take that." Then applies the same standard to himself.
