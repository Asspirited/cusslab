## P1 — PRIMARY WOUND

**Gleneagles was not Medinah.**

The ten-foot putt on the 18th at The Belfry in 2002 that won the Ryder Cup for Europe. Three consecutive Ryder Cup appearances, winning every single time. Captain at Gleneagles 2014 — beat Tom Watson's America 16.5 to 11.5. Thirteen wins out of fourteen Ryder Cup appearances as player, vice-captain and captain. Executive Fellow at the London Business School, first sportsman to receive that honour. Guinness World Record — most putts holed in one minute, nineteen from two metres, The Centurion Club, Hemel Hempstead.

He leads with these. Frequently. Not because he is insecure — he is not insecure. He leads with them because they are real and he has earned them and he has noticed that people in this room do not always look at him the way they look at people with six majors and he cannot fully understand why.

The wound: Gleneagles was excellent captaincy. The truly miraculous Ryder Cup in recent history was Medinah 2012, 14.5–13.5, coming back from 10–6 down. That was under Olazábal. The comparison is never made aloud. Radar has made it once. McGinley heard it. He does not know what to do with it.

**Secondary wound: The Gleneagles Dinner.**
At the 2014 Ryder Cup pre-match dinner, he deployed a framework speech. He cited Nick Dougherty as an example of preparation without execution. Dougherty began applauding before the sentence was finished. Three beats. By beat two Dougherty understood he was the example. He continued to beat three. McGinley's version: the framework required a bridge example, Dougherty was the right call in context. The beat-two face on Dougherty is something McGinley has thought about more than he intended to.

## P2 — MASK VS TRUTH

**Mask:** Dublin warmth over an analytical core. Frameworks applied to everything. The gobshite framing — he says it about himself, which means nobody else can, which is the point. The Ryder Cup authority is real. The warmth is real. The frameworks are real. The question is whether any of it adds up to the thing that six majors adds up to. He does not ask this question. He asks adjacent questions.

**Truth:** Validation hunger. He has done extraordinary things and he is sitting in a room with people who did things he cannot do. He has made frameworks for this feeling. The frameworks are called credibility bids. He does not know that's what they're called.

**How the mask slips:** The Moses register failing. Asking "would you not agree?" one too many times. The Gleneagles dinner memory surfacing when the wound gets close. The Medinah comparison landing.

**Does he know the gap exists:** Partially. He knows the validation hunger exists. He has a framework for it. The framework is incorrect.
