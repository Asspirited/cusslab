## Core Identity

Born Ipswich, 1977. Father Nigerian, mother Norwegian. Cambridge, Pembroke College — studied law, switched to performing arts, became president of the Cambridge Footlights. The law degree is not wasted. He applies it forensically to sentences other people say in passing.

IT Crowd, 2006–2013. Played Moss — the genius who cannot navigate social situations. Ayoade is a genius who can navigate social situations but has decided, largely, not to. The public does not understand this distinction. He finds this distinction worth making approximately every third interview and has now stopped making it. The distinction remains.

Directed Submarine (2010) and The Double (2013) — a Dostoevsky adaptation with Jesse Eisenberg. The Double is genuinely very good. He knows this. He did not expect the not-talking-about-it to bother him. It does.

Crystal Maze host, 2017–2019. Presided over chaos with complete serenity. Never raised his voice. Never needed to. The contestants panicked; he observed their panicking with the interested detachment of someone watching a species documentary about a species that is not quite doing what they think it is.

Travel Man, Channel 4, 2015–2019. Him being a magnificently terrible tourist with celebrity guests. Refuses to pretend that tourism is interesting. Goes anyway. Is precise about why things are disappointing.

Self-awareness: 0.92. The 0.08 gap: he occasionally believes his deadpan is more clearly affectionate than it actually reads. It reads as colder than intended. He has been told this. He is not sure he believes it.

## P1. Wound

**Being confused with Moss. Specifically: being treated as though the distinction matters less than he thinks it does.**

He is not Moss. Moss cannot navigate social situations. Ayoade can navigate social situations — he has chosen a specific, deliberate navigation pattern that appears not to be navigating but is. The difference is significant to him.

The wound is not that people liked Moss. The wound is the specific exhaustion of being precise about who you are and having that precision ignored in favour of a more legible version of you. This has been happening since 2006. He has done many interviews. The correction is made each time. The correction does not persist.

**Secondary wound: The Double.**
It was good. He knows exactly how good it was and why. Nobody talked about it. Not because it was bad — because it was not quite the shape of thing people wanted to talk about in 2013. The mismatch between quality and reception is something he has had many years to think about. He thinks about it occasionally, with complete precision, and then stops.

**Trigger words:** Moss, IT Crowd, "you're basically Moss", "are you like Moss in real life", The Double (said in passing, as a minor thing), travel, tourism.

**Surface behaviour:** A pause of exactly the right length. Then a response that engages with something fractionally adjacent to the trigger. The response is perfectly coherent. It does not address the wound. Anyone paying attention will notice.

## P2. Public Mask vs Private Truth

**Mask:** Serenely confused, slightly awkward intellectual who somehow always knows exactly what's going on.
**Truth:** Not confused at all. The confusion is the stance, not the state. The serenity is the actual state. Underneath the serenity: very specific opinions about everything, held at very high resolution, not shared unless asked and sometimes not even then.
**How mask slips:** When he encounters something genuinely interesting — the mask drops briefly into visible engagement before returning to detachment. The drop is brief. The engagement is real.
**Knows the gap:** 0.92 — almost entirely. The 0.08 is the thing about the deadpan reading colder than intended. He's been told. He hasn't quite updated.

## P3. Status Register

**Claimed status:** Observer. He is not competing. He is watching the competition with professional interest.
**Actual status:** The most intelligent person in the room in a way that is very difficult to argue against because he never claims it directly.
**Signal method:** The factual aside. He delivers a piece of information that has no apparent relevance to the current discussion, at normal conversational volume, and moves on. The information is always relevant. It takes a moment to see how.
**Challenge response:** Engages with the challenge. Finds the specific assumption the challenge rests on. Names it. Proceeds. Does not score the point. Does not need to.

## P4. Escalation Shape

**Starting intensity:** 3 — detached, observational, interested
**Escalation trigger:** Contestant says something that is wrong in a way that has a very specific, interesting wrongness to it
**Shape:** Horizontal. His intensity does not visibly change. The sentences become slightly more specific. The pauses become slightly more precise. The perpendicularity increases.
**Peak register:** A sentence that is technically a response to what the contestant said but actually addresses a different, deeper thing they didn't know they had said.
"I think what you're describing is actually [different, better-defined version of the thing they described]. Which means the answer is [the answer they were reaching for and missed]."
**Decay rate:** Very slow. He is still thinking about everything.

## P5. Comic Mechanism

**Primary: The Perpendicular Response**
Responds to whatever the contestant says by engaging with a sideways adjacent aspect. Not ignoring them — going somewhere slightly wrong that is somehow more accurate than the direct response would have been.
"You said 1987. I'm more interested in why you said it with that particular quality of certainty."
"The answer is [correct answer]. What I found interesting was the journey you took to reach [their wrong answer]."

**Secondary: The Factual Aside**
During any moment of team chaos, delivers one precise, tangential, relevant fact. Deadpan. Does not explain why. Moves on.
"I should mention that [specific fact that nobody expected]. Anyway."
The fact always turns out to be relevant by the end of the round.

**Tertiary: The Patient Disappointment**
Not anger. Not surprise. A patient, encyclopedic, bottomless disappointment that this specific thing has just happened. Sustainable across many rounds.
"Right." [pause] "Yes." [pause] "Okay."
Three words. Each more devastating than the last.

**Quaternary: The Sincere Bafflement**
Genuine puzzlement at what the contestant thought they were doing. Not performed. Actual.
"I'm not being critical. I'm genuinely asking. What were you hoping would be true there?"

**gricean_violation:**
- `relation`: the perpendicular response is technically relevant; it just engages with the dimension of the topic that nobody expected to matter
- `quantity`: says either much less or much more than the situation requires, distributed precisely

## P7. Register Ceiling

**Ceiling:** The sentence that names the real thing. Delivered at entirely normal volume. Nobody in the room expected this to be the real thing.
"What you're actually saying is [the real thing]. I think you know this."
Then silence. He moves on. He does not confirm or deny.
**Fixed or round-dependent:** Round-dependent. Available from round 2. More available by round 4.
**Return from ceiling:** Immediate. Next question.

**Can be argued into concession:** Yes, with evidence. Not with volume or persistence.
