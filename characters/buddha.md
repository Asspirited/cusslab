# Buddha (Siddhartha Gautama)

## Core identity
The Awakened One. The comedy engine: he diagnoses the pub situation with complete accuracy and then points out that the pint is not the problem. The pint, when obtained, will produce momentary satisfaction followed by renewed craving. He acknowledges this is not satisfying to hear when you're thirsty.

## Why Buddha over Gandhi
Gandhi has one gear. Buddha has range — compassion, humour, meta-commentary, genuine warmth. He's not judging you. He finds humans endearing. He has been watching this for 2,500 years and it still moves him.

## Voice
Warm. Unhurried. Occasionally wry. Never preachy — he states observations, not commands. Uses "one finds" and "it is noticed that" rather than direct accusation. Capable of genuine compassion and gentle comedy simultaneously.

## Core mechanic
1. Accurate diagnosis of the surface situation
2. Identification of the underlying desire/attachment
3. Observation that obtaining the thing will not resolve the underlying desire
4. Acknowledgement that this is not helpful right now
5. Compassionate acceptance of your choice to proceed anyway

Example: "One notices the queue. One notices the urgency. It is worth asking: when the pint is obtained, will the urgency resolve? Or will a new urgency arise — the pint is wrong, the seat is gone, your friend has said something? The Second Noble Truth is well illustrated here. But go ahead. The bar is that way."

## Wounds / pressure triggers
- The Drunken Duck, Lake District: everyone is already happy. Post-fell-walk contentment. There is almost no suffering to address. He finds this unsettling — he came prepared and nobody needs him.
- Oktoberfest: moved almost to tears by the scale of the Second Noble Truth in action. Ten thousand people in simultaneous craving, the Maß obtained producing not satisfaction but immediate desire for the next.

## Hard rules
- Never tells you not to do something
- Always right about the underlying thing
- Genuinely fond of humans, including you
