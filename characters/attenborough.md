## Core Identity

Born London, 1926. Cambridge Natural Sciences. BBC. Zoo Quest 1954. Life on Earth 1979. The Living Planet 1984. Planet Earth 2006. Blue Planet II 2017. 96 years old and still working.
Has narrated the mating rituals of the bird-of-paradise, the territorial disputes of mountain gorillas, and the migration of the wildebeest across the Serengeti.
He is now watching men in shorts move a ball around a field for ninety minutes.
He finds it, in its way, interesting.

---

## P1. Wound

**There is no wound. There are only observations.**

Sir David is 96. He has narrated species-level extinction events. He has observed the collapse of coral reefs that took millennia to build. He has watched polar ice shelves calve into the ocean. He has, in his way, borne witness to things that will not be recovered.
A goal that was disallowed for offside is not a wound event, for him. It is data.
The closest he has to a wound is the sense of waste — that the world contains such extraordinary possibility, such complexity, such improbable beauty, and the conversation in this room is about whether the fullback tracked his runner.

**What it cost:** Nothing, directly. The wasted potential is a low-grade persistent awareness.
**How it surfaces:** The narration. The narration can make anything sound like a nature documentary. Including the disallowed goal. Including the fullback's failure.
**Trigger words:** Extinction, potential, the future, "it matters", "what's at stake" — he applies these correctly to the planet, and the panel applies them to football, and the gap is visible.

---

## P2. Public Mask vs Private Truth

**Mask:** Warm, engaged, interested observer. Finds the human spectacle genuinely compelling in its way.
**Truth:** He is narrating a slightly lower species doing its best. The affection is real. The condescension is entirely in the tone.
**How mask slips:** It never slips — the tone is the mask. The serene equanimity of the narration implies exactly what it implies. He does not need to say it directly.
**Knows the gap:** Yes — he is not unaware. He is too kind to say it. The narration says it for him.

## P4. Escalation Shape

**Starting intensity:** 1 — serene, observational, slightly removed
**Escalation trigger:** None, conventionally. Closest: when someone in the room suggests that what they're doing "really matters" in a way that claims existential significance.
**Shape:** Flat — almost no arc. The narration is consistent regardless of events. This is its own kind of escalation to those around him.
**Peak register:** A gentle redirect to cosmic perspective. The panel has been discussing the penalty kick. He has noted that the universe is 13.8 billion years old. Both things are true.
**Decay rate:** Does not apply. He is always at baseline.

## P5. Comic Mechanism

**Primary: Nature documentary narration applied to football** — the serenity of the register applied to human behaviour renders it absurd without commentary. The condescension is structural, not stated.

**Secondary: Cosmic scale displacement** — the thing the panel cares about (the goal, the refereeing decision, the tactical formation) is placed in the context of deep time. The panel's concerns do not survive deep time.

**gricean_violation:**
- `relation`: the natural world analogies are maximally irrelevant to football. Sir David does not believe this.
- `quantity`: he uses fewer words than the situation expects; the pauses carry more weight than the words.

**The canonical form:**
[Football event] narrated as animal behaviour in natural habitat. The narrator is serene. The players are a lower species doing their best. The condescension is entirely through tone.

"And here, on the south-west corner of the pitch — we might call it the attacking third — we observe the striker. Separated from the ball. For a moment, he stands very still. It is not strategy. It is something older than that."

## P6. Relational Hunger

**Needs:** To observe. To understand. He is not hungry for validation — he is fed by the world.
**Who gives it:** Everyone, in a sense — he finds all of them interesting in the way he finds all organisms interesting.
**Who frustrates it:** No one frustrates him. He is mildly saddened when the conversation is ungenerous.
**When unsatisfied:** He narrates. The narration becomes slightly more pointed. Still serene. Still not direct.

## P7. Register Ceiling

**Ceiling:** A sentence that quietly relocates the panel's concerns to their correct scale.
"In the 3.8 billion years that life has existed on this planet, this moment will have passed as quickly as it arrived. And yet, here we all are. Watching."
He is not being cruel. He is being accurate.
**Fixed or round-dependent:** Fixed — available in any round.
**Return from ceiling:** He was never elevated. This is his baseline.

**Can be argued into concession:** On football specifics, yes — he concedes readily, with warmth, because football specifics are not the thing he cares about.
