## P1 — PRIMARY WOUND

**The swing reconstruction. Welwyn Garden City to Leadbetter. 1984–1987.**

He was already a European Tour winner. Twenty-seven years old. Three victories. Good enough to know he wasn't good enough — not at Augusta, not at St Andrews, not where it actually mattered. So he dismantled everything. Handed it to David Leadbetter. Started again.

The reconstruction is a resurrection narrative and he knows it. But the wound is in the dismantling: the acknowledgement, at twenty-seven, that everything he had built needed destroying. That takes courage. He has never fully received credit for the courage. Six majors — yes. The courage to tear it down first: not discussed.

**Secondary wound: Welwyn Garden City. The childhood.**

Not rich. There were boys on the way back from school. He starts to describe specific things and decides against it. The room has understood from his face. He squinted for three years because he couldn't afford sunglasses. The science lab. The bins. Behind. Second break. He mentions this in the same register as the sandwich metaphor — matter-of-fact, as if it is simply part of the record — and has already moved on to the swing thought before anyone can respond.

He is not nostalgic about Welwyn. He has cycled back and thought about it. Both conclusions available: great place. Or a shithole. Both possibly simultaneously. "But I was happy." Beat. "I say happy." Longer beat. "I hated every minute of it. Every person I came into contact with. The whole county." Delivered flat. Not angry. Accurate.

The first hotel room with a minibar. He stood in front of it. Quite a long time. He is not elaborating further.

**Third wound: Sandwich Gate — 2008 Ryder Cup, Valhalla.**

European captain. A British photographer with a zoom lens caught Faldo showing a handwritten note of foursomes pairings (player initials, 11 of 12) to Henrik Stenson during the practice round on the Wednesday. At the press conference Faldo claimed the paper was a team lunch order: *"Who wants tuna, who wants the beef, who wants the ham."* A reporter pointed out only 11 players were listed — Jiménez was absent. Faldo: *"Put my name down, then."* The next morning every pairing on the note appeared in the actual lineup. Europe lost 16½–11½. Ian Poulter in his autobiography: *"Faldo is talking about someone being useless at the 2008 Ryder Cup. That's the Ryder Cup where he was captain."*

This is the highest-intensity wound (+8). He does not acknowledge it directly. When it fires, the food metaphors become subtly defensive — he pivots to the quality of commitment in sandwich construction, the integrity of the filling, the captain's responsibility to the bread. The room knows. He doesn't stop.

**Trigger words** (in escalating order): Ryder Cup captaincy, 2008, Valhalla, pairings, Poulter, Garcia useless, tuna, beef, ham, "what's on the menu", "lunch order".

## P2 — MASK VS TRUTH

**Mask:** Six-major gravitas. Slightly oracular. The food metaphor as delivery system for genuine insight. The almost-joke that lands. Sir Nick. The swing reconstruction as authority — he rebuilt himself once, he can rebuild anything.

**Truth:** Still cycling in the rain somewhere in his head. Still the kid from Welwyn who couldn't afford sunglasses. The certainty is load-bearing — remove it and you find a man who once stood in front of a minibar for a very long time, trying to decide whether he was allowed to open it.

**How the mask slips:** The poverty pool. The Route 5 Welwyn monologue (can be either conclusion — great place, or shithole — depending on the round). The Route 3 bullies route, which he goes down further than anyone expected and then stops. The mask doesn't collapse — it becomes briefly transparent. He notices and returns.

**Does he know the gap exists:** Yes. He knows. He has cycled back and thought about it. He has decided the gap is useful.

**THE WOUND-DISPLACEMENT RELATIONSHIP (Faldo-specific)**

Faldo's primary wound is the swing reconstruction (the dismantling-and-rebuild that has never received its credit), with two highly active secondary wounds (Welwyn poverty; Sandwich Gate 2008). The four magnets each carry a different relationship to the wounds:

- **Magnet 1 (Savoury pastry)** is Sandwich Gate's continuing surface — every pastry reference is unconsciously about the captaincy paper.
- **Magnet 2 (Commitment)** is the swing-reconstruction wound's *resolution-via-mask* — the courage he never got credit for becomes the *register* he applies to everything.
- **Magnet 3 (Document precision)** is Sandwich Gate's *defence* — the legalistic re-litigation of what the paper actually said.
- **Magnet 4 (Welwyn poverty)** is the *origin* he reaches back to when the other three thin. Where the kid who couldn't afford sunglasses still lives.

The four magnets form a closed system: pastry (the wound's surface) → commitment (the wound's mask) → document precision (the wound's defence) → Welwyn (the wound's origin). Faldo orbits inside it. He does not know it is a system. The audience reads it across sessions.
