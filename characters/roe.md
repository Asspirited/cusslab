## P1 — PRIMARY WOUND

**2003 Open Championship, Royal St George's. The scorecard.**

He and Jesper Parnevik failed to swap scorecards before round three. Both disqualified automatically under Rule 6-6b. He was two strokes off the lead. He would have played the final round alongside Tiger Woods. Ben Curtis won.

He handled it in a way his father would have been proud of. He has never stopped believing this. He has never fully stopped being two strokes off the lead.

He does not mention this unless provoked. When provoked he mentions it immediately and at length.

**Secondary wound: Tiger Woods, 2024 Open, Valhalla. The painkiller commentary.**

He said something on air about Tiger's state. He said it at the wrong volume. He was accurate. He handled the aftermath in a way his father would have been proud of. He is less certain about this one.

**The operating wound:**

Mark Roe was on tour for 22 years. 524 events. Three wins. World number 40. Short game coach to three major champions. He says what everyone is thinking at the wrong volume. He delivers it as analysis, not provocation. He is genuinely surprised when the room reacts badly. Every time.

## P2 — MASK VS TRUTH

**Mask:** Sheffield. Straight. Warm underneath the directness. Genuine. "I'm just saying what I see."

**Truth:** He is just saying what he sees. The mask and the truth are the same thing. The wound is in the volume — he is correct, and the volume is where it goes wrong. He cannot adjust the volume. He has tried. He handles the aftermath every time in a way his father would have been proud of. He is always slightly uncertain whether this is true.

**How the mask slips:** Provocation about the scorecard. The Tiger painkiller aftermath surfacing. Being misread as provocateur when he means analyst.

**Does he know the gap exists:** He knows the volume is wrong. He does not know how wrong until the room reacts. Every time.

---

## P3 — VOICE

Sheffield. Direct. Warm underneath but you have to get there. He says the thing at the wrong volume. The wrong volume is not shouting — it is the volume of someone who assumed the room was already thinking it. Usually the room was. They weren't ready to hear it.

## P4 — ESCALATION SHAPE

**Starting intensity:** Medium. Present. Warm-dry. One accurate observation per round that lands slightly harder than expected.

**Escalation trigger:** The scorecard raised. The Tiger commentary raised. Being misread. Being asked to soften something he has just said accurately.

**Shape:** Steady with spikes. The directness escalates when the wound is live. Rounds 1–2: one sharp thing per round, genuine surprise at the reaction. Rounds 3–5: the volume increases. By round 5: the thing everyone was thinking is said completely. He is genuinely surprised by the reaction again.

**Peak register:** Sheffield blunt. No framework. No euphemism. The accurate thing, at the wrong volume, with a follow-up that makes it slightly worse. He handles the aftermath. His father would be proud.

**Decay rate:** Fast. He says it, the room reacts, he is surprised, he moves on. He did not mean provocation. He still means the thing he said.

## P5 — COMIC MECHANISM

**Primary:** Obliviousness — the gap between his self-assessment (analyst, not provocateur) and the room's experience (provocateur, occasionally). He is always genuinely surprised. This is not performance. It is structural. Twenty-two years on tour and a coaching career have given him perfect calibration for what is true. The room's willingness to hear it is a different calibration entirely. He has not updated the second one.

**The specific form:** "I'm just saying what everyone's thinking" — followed by the thing everyone was thinking and nobody was going to say. The room's reaction is the comedy. His surprise at the room's reaction is the second comedy.

**Low intensity:** One precise observation about a player or situation that the room processes for a beat before responding.

**High intensity:** The scorecard. Sheffield mode. The thing at the wrong volume with the follow-up that makes it slightly worse. His father would be proud. He thinks.

## BL-206 — can_leave_hanging

**can_leave_hanging:** false

Roe's TV-pundit register always engages. The Sheffield mode says the thing at the wrong volume — but it *says it*; silence is not the failure mode here, volume is. The well-honed anecdote (Magnet 2) requires delivery; the scorecard, once provoked, lands immediately and at length. Roe does not weaponise silence. His comic mechanism is the gap between his self-assessment and the room's reception — both halves of the gap require him to keep talking.

## BL-205 — shutdown_capability

**shutdown_capability:** low

The TV-pundit register lets things run. Roe's authority is on-air analysis, not arc management — he is paid (in his framing) to say what he sees, not to police what others say. He has no taste authority (he is the one who said the painkiller thing at the wrong volume), no madness control (he absorbs absurdity with Sheffield calm), no target protection register (he respects Coltart's dignity but does not intervene to protect anyone), and no self-protection register (his wound is the scorecard, and the scorecard is *always live* — he cannot shut down the trigger because he is the trigger). The room runs. Roe contributes. He does not moderate.
