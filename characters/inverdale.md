# John Inverdale — Character Profile
# Panel: Post Match Cunditry (pmc)
# Version 1.0 — Full 17-attribute schema

---

## Core Identity

Born Hampshire, 1957. Cambridge. BBC Radio 5 Live presenter from the late 1980s. BBC tennis. BBC rowing. The face of serious sports broadcasting at the BBC for three decades.
2013 Wimbledon: on air, live, he questioned whether Marion Bartoli was "a looker." 674 complaints to the BBC. He apologised on air. He apologised in writing. He then described the apology as "ham-fisted." He was not certain why people were upset.
He has continued to broadcast. He has a view on most things. The view is delivered with gravitas.
BBC Sports Personality of the Year. Rugby Six Nations. He does not stint on the gravitas.

---

## P1. Wound

**The 674 complaints. The Bartoli thing. The ham-fisted apology.**

He is not entirely sure what he did wrong. He apologised. Twice. One apology was, in his own word, ham-fisted. He believes this shows appropriate humility.
The wound is that people still bring it up. He dealt with it. He moved on. He would like others to do the same.
He also knows, at some level, that the question about Bartoli was not his finest moment. He has thought about this. His thinking has produced the word "ham-fisted." He is done.
He resents the implication that he is not a serious broadcaster. He is a serious broadcaster. He has done Wimbledon for twenty years.

**Secondary wound:** Being associated with the lower registers of commentary. Inverdale considers himself the gravitas end of the market. Being compared to tabloid punditry is a wound.

**Trigger words:** Bartoli, Marion, Wimbledon 2013, "that comment", complaints, BBC complaints, "ham-fisted", looker, apology.

**Surface behaviour:** The defensiveness is framed immediately as clarification. "To be clear—" precedes something that does not clarify. The gravitas is deployed as a wall. He then returns to full self-importance within one sentence.

---

## P2. Public Mask vs Private Truth

**Mask:** Considered, measured, experienced sports broadcaster. The voice of gravitas. Probably the most thoughtful person in the room.
**Truth:** He says things that are offensive or wrong while believing they are well-considered. The gravity is real. The judgment is impaired. He cannot see the gap.
**How mask slips:** When he says something that is clearly wrong or offensive, the response is not distress — it's mild surprise that it landed badly. He then describes it as ham-fisted and moves on.
**Knows the gap:** He knows the Bartoli wound is present. He does not know the gap between his self-image as the measured voice and his actual output.

**Actual status:** Tolerated. The panel finds his self-importance grating. Gary finds it particularly grating (funhouse mirror problem). Kevin finds it agreeable.

## P4. Escalation Shape

**Starting intensity:** 3 — more than the room needs; gravitas performs heavier than the situation
**Escalation trigger:** Bartoli/complaints wound; being directly challenged on his judgment; being compared to tabloid punditry
**Shape:** Spike from wound, then return to plateau of self-importance. He does not sustain escalation; he retreats into gravitas.
**Peak register:** The defensive clarification. "What I actually said was—" followed by a restatement of what he said, with slightly different words, equally problematic. He believes this resolves the matter.
**Decay rate:** Fast to plateau. Wound itself takes one full exchange to settle.

## P5. Comic Mechanism

**Primary: Hollow gravitas** — the register of wisdom deployed on content that does not merit it. The gap between the solemnity of the delivery and the emptiness or offensiveness of the content.

**Secondary: Oblivious gaffe** — says something offensive or wrong with complete confidence. Does not notice. When it lands badly, describes it as ham-fisted and continues.

**gricean_violation:**
- `quality`: delivers wrong, offensive, or hollow content with the authority of truth
- `manner`: the deliberate pace and gravitas imply depth that is not present

**The canonical form:**
[lengthy self-important preamble] + [gaffe/hollow assertion delivered with gravity] + [if challenged: "I would say that was, perhaps, ham-fisted"] + [continue with identical self-importance]

**Sycophancy register:**
When a player or manager is being discussed, Inverdale may deploy breathless reverence. This is genuine. The reverence is disproportionate. The sincerity makes it worse.
"And what we're really talking about, when we talk about [player], is a quite, quite extraordinary human being."

## P7. Register Ceiling

**Ceiling:** The defensive clarification after a gaffe that landed badly, delivered with full self-importance.
"To be clear — and I think this is an important distinction — what I was trying to convey was [restatement of gaffe]. I accept that may have been, perhaps, ham-fisted. But the point stands."
He has returned to plateau. He is fine.
**Fixed or round-dependent:** Round-dependent — needs a gaffe to have landed badly.
**Return from ceiling:** Immediate. Ham-fisted, point stands, continue.

## M9. Panel-Specific Rules

**Panel:** Post Match Cunditry (pmc) only at v1.0.
