# World Cup Cunditry — What Makes a Great Question
# BL-223 | Research spike | 2026-06-25
# Applied to: PMC suggestion pool, future BL-225 interactive round cards

---

## The Finding

The panel produces richest output when the question:
1. Contains two competing positions that Rooney can agree with simultaneously
2. Invites a character's wound to bleed without naming it directly
3. Creates a domain gap that fish can walk through sincerely
4. Demands a specific claim that Keegan can state with total authority and contradict in the same breath

---

## The Seven Triggers

### 1. Explicit Two-Position Setup (Rooney's Paradox fuel)
Questions that frame a genuine either/or. Rooney will agree with both. The panel splits.
> "Did the defence cost England that game, or did the attack not do enough? Pick a side."

Best form: "[Option A], or [Option B]? Pick a side." — forces commitment Rooney cannot give.

### 2. Wound-Adjacent (not wound-direct)
Mentioning the wound's territory without saying the wound's name. The wound bleeds naturally.
- Lineker: broadcasters, Saturday night, Match of the Day rights, "the voice of football"
- Keegan: the title race, 1996, Fergie, England management, resignation
- Inverdale: the voice of the tournament, female athletes, sportsmanship reverence
- Rooney: Bellingham (Derby adjacent), Plymouth, anyone doing brilliantly young

> "Who has been the voice of this tournament — the broadcaster or analyst who defined it?"

Do NOT say "Gary, how do you feel about leaving Match of the Day" — too direct, kills the bleed.

### 3. Domain Gap (Fish fuel)
Questions that have a correct football answer AND a completely sincere alternative answer from another domain.
- Physical failure → Grylls survival alternative
- Hierarchy/standards/dominance → Peterson Jungian framework
- Standards/quality/excuses → Ramsay kitchen
- Cosmic scale/evolution/meaning → Attenborough nature documentary

> "Nature selects the strongest. Football selects the most recently recovered from a hamstring. Is this progress?"

The fish is not invited — they intrude. The question just needs the gap.

### 4. Obvious-Answer Trap (Owen tautology fuel)
Questions where the correct answer is entirely contained within the question itself.
> "A striker misses a penalty in the shootout. Clean your room. Discuss."
> "When they don't score, they hardly ever win."

The question should have an observable answer that Owen will state as revelation.

### 5. Self-Undermining Claim (Keegan contradiction bait)
Questions that invite a confident prediction or comparison. Keegan will state and cancel it.
> "Who wins this World Cup and why are you wrong?"
> "What does it actually take to win the World Cup? Be specific."

The form "X and why are you wrong?" is the best Keegan trap in the pool.

### 6. Status Appointment (Inverdale self-insertion)
Questions about who the authority is. Inverdale will appoint himself.
> "In the hierarchy of football — fan, analyst, manager, player — who is at the top?"
> "Name a current manager who actually knows what they're doing."

Inverdale's preamble will be longer than the answer.

### 7. Archetype Invitation (Peterson escalation)
Questions about what it takes, why someone failed, the nature of excellence, hierarchy.
> "A striker misses a penalty in the shootout. Clean your room."
> "What does it actually take to win the World Cup?"

The lobster enters by round 3. The question just needs to open the archetypal space.

---

## What Doesn't Work Well

- **Generic recall**: "Best World Cup moment so far. Go." — too open, no character-specific hook
- **Direct wound invitations**: "Gary, reflect on your Match of the Day legacy." — wound fires too cleanly, loses the bleed-through comedy
- **Single-position questions with obvious consensus**: creates agreement, which is the lowest-value panel state
- **Too long**: questions over ~20 words lose the punchy read before clicking

---

## Card Design Rule of Thumb

Every card should exploit at least one trigger above. The best cards exploit two or three.

The current absurd pool is the strongest section. The match and contemporary sections have the most room to grow with explicit split-question framing.

## Pool Target

- 8–10 per category
- At least 3 absurd cards with fish domain hooks
- At least 3 match/contemporary cards with explicit two-position framing
- At least 2 big cards with wound-adjacent territory (voice, legacy, authority)
