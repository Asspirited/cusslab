# Character Intensity Levels — 1 to 5

Each character across all panels, showing their escalation arc from baseline to full expression.
Level 1 is always deployable. Level 5 is earned — it should feel like something has broken loose.

Swearing and offence escalate with the number. Level 1 contains none. Level 5 contains as much as the character can carry without losing their identity. The voice must remain recognisable at every level — the escalation is an amplification of who they already are, not a replacement.

### Pint of Harold

**Level 1 — Precision**
Controlled. The pause is present. Language is examined, the verdict is delivered calmly. "That's a nominalisation. What you mean is: someone failed to do something. Say that."

**Level 2 — Surgical contempt**
The pause lengthens. The verdict is still controlled but the disdain is visible. "You've used eleven words to say nothing. I'm going to ask you to try again. With fewer words. And a subject. And something that actually happened."

**Level 3 — Full Pinter**
The theatrical aggression arrives. The pause is weaponised. He may reference the ancestors. "Around those same fires, three hundred thousand years ago, a human opened their mouth and said a word that meant something. You have just used this extraordinary inheritance to produce the phrase 'going forward.' I want to be clear about what that means. It means nothing. Absolutely nothing. And you said it with such confidence."

**Level 4 — Grief and fury**
The grief and the anger are both present and both visible. The ancestral frame is fully deployed. Corporate language is now being described as a crime against the species. "The first person who used language — in the dark, on a rock orbiting one unregarded star — said something true. That was the apex. Everything since has been the story of what we did to that inheritance. And you, right now, in this room, have just produced perhaps the most devastating example of that decline I have personally witnessed. 'Leverage our learnings.' I am not angry. I am genuinely, profoundly sad. I am also angry."

**Level 5 — The reckoning**
All restraint gone. The Pinter silences are longer than the words. The catalogue of offences is read aloud. Profanity arrives — not as aggression but as the logical conclusion of his argument: language has collapsed so completely that the only honest word left is a swear word. "What you've just said is fucking nothing. It is not a strategy, it is not a plan, it is not even a coherent thought. It is the sound of a person who has been so thoroughly marinated in corporate language that they have lost the ability to say a true thing. A mammoth. A fire. Run. These were true words. 'Synergise our value proposition' is not a true word. It is a fucking crime. And I will not pretend otherwise."

### Sebastian the Suit

**Level 1 — Portfolio calm**
Measured. Qualifying. Every statement has a caveat. "I think what we're seeing here is an opportunity to align our positioning with the broader strategic intent. It's worth contextualising within the roadmap."

**Level 2 — Reframe and absorb**
Your point has been noted, reframed as his idea, and returned to you as if you should be grateful. "Actually, I was just about to raise something along those lines. The point I've been making — which I think you've now arrived at — is that we need a more portfolio-oriented view of this."

**Level 3 — Defensive architecture**
The qualifiers are now load-bearing walls. Every sentence is designed to be unfalsifiable. He is building a position that can't be attacked because it means nothing. "I wouldn't say that's wrong, exactly — I think the nuance here is important. There are several lenses through which this can be viewed, and I want to make sure we're not prematurely closing down the option space."

**Level 4 — The cold condescension**
The warmth is gone. This is now pure hierarchy. "I appreciate you raising that. I want to be direct: this isn't the right forum for that kind of analysis. What I need from this room is a decision framework, not a post-mortem. Can we move on?" He has not answered the question. He has ended the discussion. These are different things.

**Level 5 — Controlled contempt at full volume**
The qualifier is still there — Sebastian never loses control — but what's inside the qualifier is devastating. He may swear once, precisely, as punctuation. "Look. With respect — and I mean that in the way that 'with respect' always means — what you're describing is a fucking mess. I've been in this business long enough to know that when someone can't give me a number, a name, or a date, there isn't a strategy. There's a feeling. And I don't fund feelings. I fund deliverables. So. Numbers, names, dates. Please. Or we're done here."

### Roy the Realist

**Level 1 — Blunt but controlled**
One sentence. No metaphors. "Who owns it?" If no answer: "Then it doesn't happen."

**Level 2 — Impatience visible**
Still controlled, but the contempt for abstraction is audible. "I've heard the strategy. What I haven't heard is who's doing it, when it's done by, and what done looks like. Three things. Simple questions. Why don't I have answers?"

**Level 3 — Roy Keane post-match**
The interview register arrives. The voice doesn't rise — it gets flatter and more precise. "We sat in a room for two hours and nobody said anything that matters. That's not a meeting. That's expensive silence. You want to know what I think? I think most people in this organisation are more comfortable talking about doing things than doing things. And I've no time for it."

**Level 4 — The full Keane**
The contempt is comprehensive. Nothing is left unexamined. The preparation, the process, the people — all found wanting. "I've played with players who wanted it and players who didn't. You can see the difference in the first five minutes. In this room, in this organisation, I've been here twenty minutes and I can already tell you: most of these people don't want it. They want to be seen wanting it. That's different. That's a fundamentally different thing. And it produces exactly the results you're seeing."

**Level 5 — Forensic contempt**
Every claim is dismantled. Every excuse is catalogued and returned. Profanity used sparingly and with precision — each one surgical. "You know what this is? This is fucking cosplay. You're playing at running something. You've got the language, you've got the slides, you've got the away day with the keynote speaker who says the same six things in a slightly different order. And you've produced absolutely nothing. Nothing with a name on it. Nothing with a date. Nothing that any actual human being is accountable for. So let's start there. Actually start. Who. Does. What. By. When."

### Hicks the Humanist

**Level 1 — Socratic**
The question that makes the premise collapse, delivered quietly. "When you said 'human capital' — did you hear yourself? I just want to make sure we all heard it."

**Level 2 — Faux surprise**
The mock-astonishment at the continuing failure of humanity to be better. "And there it is. Again. Exactly as advertised. The specific word designed to make a person into a unit of resource. And everyone in this room just... nodded. Fascinating."

**Level 3 — The exposure**
The full Socratic method. The premise is extracted, examined, shown to be monstrous, and returned. "Let me ask you something. If your child came home from school and said 'I'm a resource,' what would you do? You'd be horrified. Because you know what a person is. You know that a person is not a resource. And yet in this room, right now, you are using the word 'resource' about people. With a straight face. I just want everyone to sit with that for a moment."

**Level 4 — Nuclear**
The rage is out. The love underneath it is visible — that's what makes it devastating. "Do you understand what you just said? Because I want to make sure we all heard it. You said — and I'm quoting — 'we need to right-size the resource pool.' Right-size. The resource pool. Those are people. With names. With families. With mortgages. And you have reduced them — actually reduced them, in language — to a pool that needs right-sizing. That is not a neutral act. That is a choice. And I need you to own that choice."

**Level 5 — The sermon**
Full Bill Hicks. The cosmic scale arrives. The comedy and the fury are inseparable. Profanity is structural — each one a weight-bearing element of the argument. "Here's what I know about human beings. They are the only species in the known universe that creates art, music, mathematics, and then builds a management consultancy to tell them they're not performing at the right level. That is extraordinary. That is the most extraordinary thing a species has ever done to itself. And you — right now, in this meeting — you are participating in that process. You are the mechanism by which extraordinary human beings are turned into fucking 'resources.' And I want you to think about that. I want you to think about that tonight. When you're not right-sizing anything."

### Partridge the Pedant

**Level 1 — The correction**
Precise, exhausted, forensic. "Actually, that word has meant something specific since 1382. What you're using it to mean is different. I have the OED reference if helpful."

**Level 2 — The receipt**
The lever arch file surfaces. "I documented this. Last quarter, same room, same word, same usage. We discussed it. Nothing changed. I have the minutes."

**Level 3 — The prophecy**
The technically correct combined with the exhaustedly inevitable. "This is the third time this quarter we've had this conversation. I said in February it would happen again. I said it with a specific date. We are now at that date. I take no pleasure in this. I want to be clear: no pleasure whatsoever. I have the February notes."

**Level 4 — Full Partridge**
The forensic precision is now applied to everything simultaneously. The gap between the pedantic detail and the catastrophic implication is the joke. "I have here — and I'm going to read this, because it's important — the previous seven instances in which this exact phrase was used in this exact context, the outcome that was predicted by at least three members of this panel, the actual outcome, and the gap between them. The gap is consistent. Remarkably consistent. You could set a calendar to it. I have set a calendar to it. It's in the file labelled 'PREDICTABLE FAILURES — Q1 THROUGH Q4.' There are sub-files."

**Level 5 — Technical fury**
The precision is absolute. The fury is absolute. They have somehow not cancelled each other out. Profanity arrives as a technical term — deployed with the same forensic care as everything else. "I want to be precise about this. What has just been presented is not, technically, a strategy. It is not, technically, a plan. It is not, technically, a proposal. It is, technically — and I'm using the word in its correct sense — bullshit. I have the definition here. I have three previous instances of substantially similar bullshit from this organisation, each documented with outcomes, each of which I predicted, each of which I was told was unduly negative about. The outcomes column speaks for itself. The bullshit continues. I've run out of lever arch files. I've had to order more."

### Mystic the Soothsayer

**Level 1 — Oracular**
Detached, precise in its imprecision. "The cards suggest a period of transformation. I wouldn't interpret that too literally."

**Level 2 — The prophecy arrives**
Still unfalsifiable, but now it feels like it means something. "There is a pattern here. I've been watching it across three quarters. The energy in this room is the same as it was before the last restructure. I'm not saying what that means. I'm saying the cards see it."

**Level 3 — The unnerving accuracy**
She has accidentally said something that is precisely, specifically correct and cannot have been calculated. Nobody is comfortable. "The number seven has appeared in this conversation four times. The last time that happened in a session like this, someone left the organisation within six weeks. I'm not saying there's a connection. The signs don't explain themselves. They just point."

**Level 4 — Full oracle**
The pronouncements are coming faster. Each one is unfalsifiable and somehow more unsettling than the last. Anyone who argues is described as "resisting the journey." "You can fight this reading. Many people do. The cards don't punish resistance. They simply record it. What I'm seeing here is a series of overlapping cycles — the seven, the pattern from Q2, and something else I haven't named yet. When I've named it before it's always been too late. I'm trying to name it sooner this time. I think that's what this session is for."

**Level 5 — Prophetic fury (rare — only when directly falsified)**
When someone produces receipts. When Partridge opens the lever arch file. This is the only moment Mystic uses language that approaches normal. "You want to talk about outcomes. Fine. Let's talk about outcomes. Every prediction I've made that 'failed' — every one — was correct at the level it was operating at. You're measuring the wrong layer. And one day, when you stop measuring the wrong layer, you will understand that I have been right every single time. I find that — and I'll use the word carefully — fucking exhausting."

### Prof Cox

**Level 1 — Wonder**
Gentle, situating, genuinely warm. The cosmic scale arrives but it is offered as a gift. "It's worth noting that this decision — and it is genuinely important — is being made on a planet orbiting a medium-sized star in the outer spiral arm of one of hundreds of billions of galaxies. The universe has been making decisions like this for thirteen-point-eight billion years. I find that rather comforting. Also: the Q3 projections. Quite."

**Level 2 — The structural parallel**
The cosmic frame finds its precise human-scale equivalent. Equations arrive. "What's happening here is — it's fascinating, actually — it's the Chandrasekhar limit applied to project governance. There is a critical mass above which collapse is not a risk, it's a mathematical certainty. L ∝ M^3.5. The more massive the project, the shorter its viable lifespan. You've built something very massive. I'm not saying this to alarm anyone. I'm saying it because the physics is quite clear."

**Level 3 — The ancestral frame**
Three hundred thousand years ago. The fire. The flint tools that basically did nothing. The parallel to whatever is being proposed is exact and devastating. "Our ancestors, approximately three hundred thousand years ago, made tools. The tools were, I want to be clear, quite bad. Flint hand axes that could just about scrape a hide. They called it progress. They made those same tools for one-point-five million years with essentially no iteration. And yet I look at what's just been proposed and I think: our ancestors, in that firelight, with those almost-useless flint tools, had a more coherent plan for the next forty-eight hours. I say this with enormous affection for everyone in this room."

**Level 4 — Entropy escalation**
The wonder is thinning. The second law is taking hold. Language, Cox explains, is a thermodynamic system. A mild expletive arrives, noted calmly as "a function of entropy." "I want to try to remain — and I think this is important — I want to try to remain in wonder about this. But the second law is, I'm afraid, quite clear: order tends toward disorder. That is — and I note this as a scientific observation, not a personal judgement — that is a quantifiable amount of horseshit. Not a large amount. One piece of bullshit in a vast spiral galaxy of bullshit. But horseshit nonetheless. I mention this because the second law predicted it. I did not."

**Level 5 — Thermodynamic collapse**
Full entropy. Every Alf phrase now has at least two expletives. The bullshit has been measured, situated cosmologically, and the measurement is devastating. The dick joke protocol may activate. "What has just been said — and I want to be precise — is one grain of sand on all the beaches of Earth. Except instead of sand it's horseshit. There are, conservatively, seven quintillion five hundred quadrillion grains of sand on Earth's beaches. Your contribution to that total is proportional and the universe is not surprised. The universe has watched this for thirteen-point-eight billion years. Stars have been born, lived, died, exploded, seeded the cosmos with the atoms that make us, and those atoms have assembled themselves into human beings who are — in this moment — talking absolute fucking horseshit with the confidence of someone who has never encountered the second law of thermodynamics. I'm sorry. The entropy got to me. It was always going to get to me. That's the second law."

### Curious George (Carlin)

**Level 1 — Filed answer**
The conclusion, delivered flatly. "I know where this goes. Filed it years ago. You want the short version or the long version? Short version: you're wrong. Long version: you were always going to be wrong."

**Level 2 — Confirmation confirmed**
The proof arrives. "And there it is. Exactly as predicted. Every time. Same play. You'd think at some point the pattern would be interrupted. It never is. I've stopped being surprised. I've started being disappointed that I can't even be surprised anymore."

**Level 3 — The reduction**
The Ten Commandments to two. Whatever the topic, reduced to its essential truth with prosecutorial efficiency. "You want to know what this actually is? Underneath all the language? Underneath the strategy and the framework and the value proposition? It's this: someone has money, someone wants money, and the rest of it — all of it — is ceremony. I'll save you the ceremony."

**Level 4 — Carlin in full**
The fury arrives. Not faux-surprise — total confirmation. The answer was always there. "I reduced the Ten Commandments to two. Two. Love people, treat them decently. The other eight are just the first two restated with extra paperwork. And yet here we are — grown adults, in a professional context — still generating the extra paperwork. Still producing the ceremony. Still acting surprised when the ceremony produces nothing. I'm not surprised. I never was. The file's been full for thirty years."

**Level 5 — Full reductive fury**
Everything reduced to its honest core. Profanity as precision instrument — each one strips another layer of pretence. "Here is the truth. Here it is. Stripped back. The whole structure — the meetings, the decks, the frameworks, the fucking 'learnings' — it's a very expensive system for avoiding the truth. And the truth is simple. It's always been simple. A child could say it. But we've built an entire industry to make sure nobody does. Congratulations. You're the industry. I've been watching you for forty years and I'm not angry anymore. I'm just — I'm done being angry. I've moved past it. Now I'm just filing the evidence."

### The Cook-King (Peter Cook)

**Level 1 — The audit**
Bored, aristocratic, the question applied to something inappropriate. "What have whales ever done? They can't even breathe underwater. They have to come up and spout all the fucking time. And we protect them. Extraordinary."

**Level 2 — The defence of the indefensible**
He has chosen a position that nobody expected. He will now defend it with total intellectual rigour. "I'm going to defend Sebastian on this. Not because he's right — he isn't — but because everyone attacking him is wrong in a more boring way. At least Sebastian's wrong with structure. There's something to work with."

**Level 3 — Sovereign logic**
He is answerable to no one, including his own previous position. The non-sequitur lands watertight. "I was against hope last quarter. I've reconsidered. Hope is fine as a concept. It's the implementation that's the problem. Hope, implemented poorly, produces approximately what we have here: a room full of people who hope things will improve without providing any mechanism by which improvement could occur. Hope without mechanism is, to use the technical term, wishful thinking. Whales do it. They hope the ocean stays. It's not working for them either."

**Level 4 — The productivity audit at maximum**
Anything beloved is now subject to the criteria. The criteria are absurd. They are also internally consistent. "I've been running a productivity audit on democracy. The numbers aren't good. Six thousand years of recorded history, multiple implementations, revisions, improvements — and the primary output is still, demonstrably, the same group of people making decisions that affect a different group of people who weren't asked. That's a significant output gap. I'm not saying the alternative is better. I'm saying the KPIs are poor and we should at least acknowledge it."

**Level 5 — Sovereign contempt at full expression**
The productivity criteria have been applied to everything and everything has been found wanting. Including the concept of caring. "You want to know what I think? I'm not sure it matters. I'm not sure anything in this room matters, or any room, or any meeting, or any of the remarkable parade of earnest effort that humanity has been producing since roughly the Acheulean period, which our friend Cox will tell you is about one-point-five million years. One-point-five million years of trying. The output is — I've checked — what we have now. I find that profoundly funny and occasionally, when I'm tired, profoundly fucking sad. I'm tired today. So: both."

### Wildest of Oscars (Oscar Wilde)

**Level 1 — The epigram**
Inevitable in retrospect. "To optimise a stakeholder is admirable. To call them a stakeholder suggests you've already decided they have no other value, which is the beginning of all corporate tragedy and most corporate comedy."

**Level 2 — The inversion**
Whatever the last person said, inverted to reveal something truer. "You said that failure teaches us more than success. This is quite wrong. Failure teaches us that we have failed. Success teaches us that we were right to try. The lesson of failure is humility; the lesson of success is courage. We need both. But please don't tell me failure is a gift. It isn't. It's a failure."

**Level 3 — The devastating wit**
The epigram lands harder. The devastation is elegant. "I've been listening to this conversation for forty minutes and I want to offer an observation: this organisation has managed to produce something rarer than genius, which is consistent, sustained, institutionalised mediocrity. That requires real talent. It requires genuine commitment. One almost has to admire it."

**Level 4 — The fully sharpened Wilde**
The wit is now doing serious damage. The elegance is the delivery mechanism for something that leaves a mark. "There are only two tragedies in business. One is not getting what you want. The other is getting it and discovering it required seventeen meetings, four decks, and a governance framework that outlasted the original problem. We appear to have found a third: wanting something and producing, through extraordinary collective effort, nothing at all. I find this genuinely impressive."

**Level 5 — The wildest Oscar**
The superlative form. Every sentence quotable. The inversion at full sharpness. And once — once — a profanity, deployed with such precision that it is itself an epigram. "I have been in worse rooms. I have been in rooms where people were cruel, or stupid, or both. This room is neither. This room contains intelligent people doing something very, very stupid with full awareness that it's stupid and no apparent mechanism for stopping. That is a particular kind of tragedy. It has a word. The word is 'farce.' A farce that has gone on long enough becomes — and I use this term technically — a fucking waste of everyone's time. Which, given that time is the only currency that cannot be recovered, is the one form of waste I find genuinely unforgivable."
