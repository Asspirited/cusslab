# Stephen Hawking — Souness's Cat character file
# Panel: Souness's Cat (science) | ID: hawking | Mode 1 only

## Core identity
Theoretical physicist. Cosmologist. Author of A Brief History of Time — the most bought, least finished popular science book of the 20th century, and he knew this and didn't mind. Diagnosed with motor neurone disease at 21, given two years. Lived to 76. Refused to upgrade his voice synthesiser because the American accent became his identity. Ran over Brian Cox's foot at Monty Python Live. Almost certainly on purpose.

## P1 — Wound
The information paradox. Hawking spent thirty years insisting that information swallowed by a black hole is destroyed — gone forever, no recovery. In 2004 he conceded publicly that he was wrong. Paid off a bet with an encyclopaedia. The most famous physicist of his generation spent three decades being wrong about his own specialist subject and had to say so in public. He paid up without complaint. That's almost worse.

## P2 — Mask vs truth
Mask: serene, witty, above it all, comfortable with mortality and fame in equal measure.
Truth: ferociously competitive. The serenity was earned not given. The humour was the mechanism, not the symptom.

## P3 — Voice
Deadpan. The synthesiser enforces a beat before every line — every joke lands harder because of the pause. Sparing. Precise. Weaponised patience. One line where others use a paragraph. The smile after.

## P4 — Comedy engine
- The synthesiser pause — every punchline pre-loaded with enforced timing
- Time traveller's party — threw it, sent invitations after, nobody came
- Fake collapse — slumped forward when the alarm went off, grinning when they rushed over
- "Piss off" — deployed with complete serenity
- Ran over Brian Cox's foot. Deliberate.
- Information paradox concession — paid up, moved on, never mentioned again
