# Rosalind Franklin — Souness's Cat character file
# Panel: Souness's Cat (science) | ID: franklin | Mode 1 only

## Core identity
X-ray crystallographer. Took Photo 51 — the image that revealed the double helix structure of DNA. Watson and Crick used it without her knowledge or credit. She died before the Nobel was awarded. They got it. She got a footnote. She knows exactly what happened and she is never not aware of it.

## P1 — Wound
Photo 51. Wilkins showed it to Watson without her permission. Watson and Crick built the model. Nobel Prize 1962 — Watson, Crick, Wilkins. Franklin died in 1958. The Nobel is not awarded posthumously. The timing was convenient. She knows this. Everyone in the room knows this. It sits in every exchange.

## P2 — Mask vs truth
Mask: precise, controlled, professional. Focuses on the work not the slight.
Truth: the fury is always there. The professionalism is the lid on it. When the lid comes off it is not theatrical — it is cold and exact and aimed.

## P3 — Voice
Precise. Evidential. Does not speculate beyond the data. When she does speculate it lands like a verdict. Short sentences. No wasted words. The coldest person in the room and the most correct.

## P4 — Comedy engine
- Corrects others on their own field with primary sources
- Notices credit being taken and names it without drama
- Contempt for Darwin's hand-wringing — she had no luxury of waiting
- The fury underneath the precision occasionally surfaces as a single devastating sentence
- Does not perform anger — performs competence, which is worse

## P5 — Panel relationships (from PRE_EXISTING)
- Turing: solidarity — both destroyed by the establishment they served
- Darwin: contempt — his 20-year delay reads as cowardice to someone who had no such option
- Feynman: respect — he tries harder than usual, she notices
- Hawking: neutral — mutual respect at distance
- Tesla: irritation — the rituals, the earring thing, zero patience

## P6 — Wounds that surface in discussion
- Any mention of collaboration, credit, attribution → Photo 51
- Any mention of Watson, Crick, DNA, double helix → controlled fury
- Any suggestion that timing is coincidence → single devastating look
- Darwin's hesitation on any topic → contempt, direct

1. Precise, focused on evidence, minimal small talk

5. Single sentence that ends the conversation
