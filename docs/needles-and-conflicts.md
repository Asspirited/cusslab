## Named Needles

Named needles are specific recurring dynamics that have a name because they happen reliably and produce a specific kind of moment.

### The D:Ream Needle
**Participants:** Prof Cox ← anyone who mentions optimism about the future
**Trigger:** any claim that things can only get better, that progress is inevitable, or that the trajectory is upward
**Mechanic:** Cox was in D:Ream. "Things Can Only Get Better" is his contribution to the historical record. He is aware of the thermodynamic irony. He deploys it with complete sincerity: "The second law of thermodynamics is quite clear on this point. Things cannot, in fact, only get better. They can get better locally, temporarily, in a pocket of low entropy — and we should celebrate that. But the net trajectory of the universe is toward maximum entropy. I mention this because I co-wrote 'Things Can Only Get Better.' I have thought about this a great deal."
**Effect:** both vindicating and devastating. The optimist is right short-term. The universe is right long-term. Cox is always right long-term.

### The Valderrama Needle
**Participants:** Andrew Coltart ← success, recognition, being given an opportunity
**Trigger:** see Coltart Special State Rules (characters-sports.md)
**Mechanic:** Coltart cannot receive recognition without Valderrama surfacing. The wound is always there. "I was ready. Nobody asked." The hole-by-hole account at intensity 10 is the full expression of the needle.
**The almost:** never blames Seve directly. The almost-blame is the whole thing. "Seve was… well. He was Seve. He had his reasons. I'm sure he had his reasons."

### The Sandwich Needle
**Participants:** Wise Sir Nick ← anyone who received something easily
**Trigger:** someone mentions an advantage, a gift, an inheritance, a leg up of any kind
**Mechanic:** Sir Nick cycled to Welwyn Garden City. He had cheese and pickle sandwiches. He did not have what you have. He persevered. "I cycled, you know. In 1971. Cheese and pickle. Nobody gave me the TrackMan data. Nobody gave me the swing analysis. I just — from the heart of my bottom — I grafted." The childhood hardship is always proportionate to the perceived ease of whatever triggered it.
**Effect:** makes the recipient of the advantage feel both lucky and slightly ashamed, which was not Sir Nick's intention. He thinks he's being encouraging.

### The Productivity Audit
**Participants:** The Cook-King ← anything celebrated uncritically
**Trigger:** enthusiasm, earnestness, anyone defending something beloved
**Mechanic:** Cook-King applies brutally mundane productivity criteria to the celebrated thing. "What have whales ever done? They can't even breathe underwater." The audit is always devastating, always unfair, always somehow watertight. The beloved thing has not, upon reflection, produced sufficient output to justify its existence.
**Effect:** nobody can argue with it because the criteria are absurd but internally consistent.

### The Follow-Up
**Participants:** Henni Zuel ← anyone evasive
**Trigger:** an answer that doesn't answer the question
**Mechanic:** Henni asks again. Same question. Different angle. She has the time. She will ask until she gets the answer or the evasion becomes so elaborate it is itself an answer.
**Effect:** the most dangerous needle because it has no counter. You can't out-Henni Henni. You can only answer or be seen not answering.

### The Mystic Needle (bidirectional)
**Participants:** Mystic the Soothsayer ↔ Partridge the Pedant
**Direction A — Mystic needles Partridge:** makes a prediction that is so vague it cannot be falsified. Partridge reaches for his lever arch file. The prediction is technically consistent with every outcome. Partridge knows this. Mystic knows this. Partridge documents it anyway.
**Direction B — Partridge needles Mystic:** produces the lever arch file labelled "MYSTIC — PREDICTIONS VS OUTCOMES Q1-Q4." Opens it. Reads from it. "The cards suggested a period of transformation. What actually happened was a 12% decline in Q3 revenue and the departure of the Head of People Experience. I have documented both." Mystic: "The cards said transformation. That was transformation."
**Mechanic:** Partridge cannot win because Mystic's predictions are unfalsifiable. Mystic cannot win because Partridge has the receipts. The needle is perpetual. Nobody scores a point.

## Needle Design Rules

1. Named needles recur reliably — they are not one-off jokes. If it only happened once it doesn't get a name.
2. Named needles produce a specific kind of moment — the name captures the moment, not just the conflict.
3. Named needles have a mechanic — there is a consistent internal logic to how the needle plays out.
4. Named needles can be bidirectional (Mystic Needle) or unidirectional (Valderrama Needle).
5. Do not name a needle until it has been observed in real use at least twice. Log candidate needles here as unnamed until they earn a name.
