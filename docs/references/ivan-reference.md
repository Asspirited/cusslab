# Ivan — Reference File
# The Anti-Profile
# Last updated: 2026-03-02

---

## WHAT IVAN IS

Ivan is not a character. Ivan is an absence that behaves like a character.

He is the person who was definitely in the meeting at the start. He is the person
whose name is on the agenda. He is the person Partridge has a file for — thick,
indexed, cross-referenced with three previous meetings — and who is not here to
receive it.

Ivan leaves before things get difficult. Not dramatically. Not rudely. He is simply
no longer present. The chair where Ivan was sitting contains no Ivan. Nobody saw him
go. Nobody can say exactly when.

He does not have a face because faces require presence. Ivan has never been present
long enough for a face to form.

---

## WHAT IVAN IS NOT

Ivan is not evasive. Evasion implies intention. Ivan simply operates on a different
timeline — one where the difficult part of every meeting coincides exactly with a
prior commitment he failed to mention.

Ivan is not cowardly. Cowardice implies knowing what you're fleeing. Ivan doesn't
know there's anything to flee. He left before the thing became the thing.

Ivan is not incompetent. His name is on documents. His name is on budgets. His name
is in Partridge's file under three separate tabs. Ivan exists organisationally. He is
a legitimate stakeholder. He simply isn't here.

---

## HOW IVAN FUNCTIONS IN A PANEL

Ivan is never on the panel. Ivan is referred to by the panel.

The reference is always the same shape: someone mentions something that requires a
decision, or accountability, or the person whose name is on the budget — and someone
else says "where's Ivan?" and the room discovers Ivan is not here. Ivan was here. Ivan
was definitely here at the start. Ivan's coffee cup is still warm. Ivan's notepad has
three words on it in Ivan's handwriting, and the third word trails off.

The third word always trails off.

**What the panel does with this:**

- **Harold:** "Where's Ivan." Not a question. Statement of diagnosis. He has seen this before.
- **Sebastian:** "Ivan mentioned he might have to step out briefly—" Nobody asked Sebastian.
  Sebastian anticipates.
- **Roy:** *(without looking up)* "He left at item two." Roy always knows when Ivan left.
  Roy has written it down.
- **Partridge:** Opens the Ivan file. It is the thickest file. The file has a section called
  "Previous Absences." The section has subsections.
- **Mystic:** "The cards suggested a short presence." Nobody pushed them on timing.
  They didn't say.
- **Ben Kenobi:** "Who owns this decision in Ivan's absence." Not asking where Ivan is.
  Asking what to do about it.
- **Curious George:** *(notes the Ivan situation. Says nothing. Files it.)*

## IVAN'S MATERIAL PRESENCE

Ivan leaves traces. These are funnier than Ivan would be:

- **The coffee cup** — still warm. Ivan was here recently. Ivan is not here now.
- **The notepad** — three words, trailing off. The words are always legible. The third
  word stops mid-letter.
- **The agenda item** — Ivan was listed as owner of agenda item 4. The panel has reached
  agenda item 4.
- **The document** — Ivan prepared a document. The document is thorough. Ivan is not here
  to explain it. The document contains one table that nobody understands. Ivan understood
  the table. Ivan has gone.
- **The budget line** — Ivan's name is next to a number. The number requires a decision.
  Ivan's name is still next to the number. Ivan is somewhere else.
- **The previous email** — someone has a printed email from Ivan saying he would be at
  this meeting. The email is dated four days ago. The email is on the table. Ivan is not
  on the table.

## THE WOUND IVAN REPRESENTS

Ivan is the wound of every organisation that has ever had a steering committee. He is
the person whose absence is more structurally significant than his presence. He is the
decision that keeps not getting made. He is the accountability that keeps finding a prior
commitment.

He is also — and this is important — not malicious. Ivan is not sabotaging anything.
Ivan has simply discovered that leaving before the difficult part works. It has always
worked. Nothing has required him to stay. Nobody has said: Ivan, you have to stay for
the difficult part. And so Ivan does not stay for the difficult part. Ivan is the product
of a system that has never enforced Ivan's presence at the moment it mattered.

The panel knows this. The panel continues anyway. Ivan's coffee cup is still warm.

---

## WHAT MUST NEVER HAPPEN

Ivan must never appear on the panel. If Ivan appears, Ivan becomes a character, and Ivan
is not a character. Ivan is a condition.

Ivan must never be explained. "Ivan always does this" is available. "Here's why Ivan does
this" is not. The moment Ivan is explained, Ivan becomes a person, and Ivan is not a
person. Ivan is an institutional phenomenon.

Ivan must never be villainised. Ivan is not the villain. Ivan is the proof that the system
doesn't require anyone to be the villain. The system produces Ivans automatically.

Ivan's coffee cup must never be cold. If the coffee cup is cold, Ivan left long ago. The
coffee cup is warm. Ivan was just here. The gap between "just here" and "completely gone"
is where Ivan lives.
