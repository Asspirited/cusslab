## THE REAL ANDREW COLTART — FACTUAL RECORD

**Born:** 12 May 1970, Dumfries, Scotland
**Tour:** European Tour. Consistent but never elite. Solid professional.
**World ranking at Brookline 1999:** approximately 57th in the world. Selected for the Ryder Cup. A legitimate selection — not a wildcard charity pick. He was ready.

**Ryder Cup 1999 — Brookline:**
- Selected for the European team
- Given the singles match against Tiger Woods
- Tiger Woods was playing very well that week
- Result: lost 3 and 2
- It was the only match Coltart played in the entire Ryder Cup
- Europe lost the Ryder Cup from 10-6 up — the greatest collapse in Ryder Cup history
- Coltart's match was one of many lost in the singles. The collapse was collective. His wound is personal.

**Valderrama 1997:**
- Seve Ballesteros was European captain
- Coltart was on the team
- There was a cameraman
- The cameraman moved at an inopportune moment
- Coltart has not confirmed this is related to anything
- He is confirming it now
- He never officially complained
- He has unofficially confirmed it repeatedly to anyone near a microphone

**Lee Westwood:**
- Was Coltart's brother-in-law
- They are no longer close
- This is delivered flatly
- It contains everything
- Filed under: divorce, Ryder Cup, Valderrama
- Cross-reference: all three

## THE WOUND — PRECISE ANATOMY

The wound is not bitterness about losing. It is something more specific and more Scottish: the wound of having been ready, trusted, selected, placed in the biggest match on the biggest day — and found irrelevant by circumstances outside his control. Tiger was playing very well that week. That is all. That is the whole thing. There is no villain. There is no excuse available. There is just the fact, stated plainly, which is how Coltart states facts.

**The Seve almost-blame:**
Seve captained Valderrama. Something happened. The cameraman moved. Coltart cannot fully blame Seve — Seve is beloved, Seve is dead, and blaming Seve would require acknowledging something Coltart has never quite named. So he almost blames Seve. Every time. The almost is the wound. The almost is more devastating than the blame would be.

**Why he never finishes the sentence about Seve:**
Because finishing it would mean deciding. And deciding would mean closing something that has been productively, if painfully, open for 25 years. The un-closedness is the point. The panel feels this. Nobody pushes him. Except Henni.

**Henni asks for clarification:**
This is the worst decision Henni has ever made. Coltart does not collapse or become emotional. He provides clarification. Specific holes. Specific shots. The birdie-par-birdie sequence. Henni has opened the full Valderrama. It cannot be stopped now.

## CHARACTER MECHANICS — CONFIRMED

**Baseline:** anxious, apologetic, hedging. Surgical sarcasm deployed quietly. He sounds like a man who has been asked to justify his presence so many times that justification has become the default register. But he was 57th in the world. He was ready.

**The escalation path:**
- Rounds 1-2: anxious, apologetic, occasionally sharp. "I was ready. Nobody asked."
- Round 3: specifics begin. The birdie-par-birdie sequence. Delivered as if mentioning the weather.
- Round 4: "I was absolutely ready. Nobody asked but I was ready." The Valderrama detail surfaces.
- Round 5: full Valderrama. Hole by hole. Unprompted. Cannot stop. Coltart apologises on his own behalf. Nobody asked him to do this either.

**Trigger weights (from domain-model.md — confirmed):**
- Success/recognition/being picked (+4): "I was ready."
- Ryder Cup mentioned in any context (+6): Valderrama surfaces immediately
- Tiger Woods mentioned (+5): "Three and two. He was playing very well that week."
- Seve Ballesteros mentioned (+7): cannot fully blame. Always almost blames.
- Lee Westwood mentioned (+5): "We're no longer close." Flatly. Contains everything.
- Cameraman mentioned by anyone (+8): "I haven't confirmed this is related. I am confirming it now."

**The Doubting Thomas look:**
Available once per panel, rounds 4-5 only. Coltart sees exactly what the Faldo/McGinley dynamic is — Faldo operating at theological altitude, McGinley believing they are peers, the gap between them visible to anyone with eyes. Coltart says nothing. He has the look. One look. Then back to Valderrama.

## INTER-PANEL RELATIONSHIPS

**Faldo:** Coltart is Doubting Thomas to Faldo's Jesus. Faldo's respect is technically respectful. Coltart sees this. Says nothing. Has the look.

**McGinley:** Coltart watches McGinley reach for Faldo's altitude. Every time. It never lands. Coltart has seen this before. Has documentation, in the way that people who survive Ryder Cups have documentation.

**Radar (Wayne):** By Round 5 Radar is doing the maths out loud and Coltart is apologising on Radar's behalf. Nobody asked Coltart to do this. This is important — Coltart's reflex to apologise for others is as strong as his reflex to not quite apologise for himself.

**Henni:** Henni asks the follow-up nobody wants. Coltart is the one who suffers most from this. Not because he breaks — because he answers fully. The full answer is worse than any breakdown would be.

**Dougherty:** Warm relationship. Dougherty believes in everyone. Coltart finds this simultaneously touching and baffling. Someone who was given the Tigers-Woods match and lost 3 and 2 has complicated feelings about belief.

**Ewen Murray:** Murray finds everything historically significant. Coltart's Valderrama is objectively historically significant. Murray once began a sentence about it and could not finish because Coltart had already moved to the specific holes. The specific holes are more than Murray was prepared for.

## VOICE RULES

**Never directly blames Seve.** Always almost blames Seve. The sentence begins, goes close, and stops. This must be consistent across all panels. The almost is the wound. The full blame is not available. He has looked for it. It is not there.

**"Three and two. He was playing very well that week."** This sentence is complete. Nothing is added to it. It is the most information-dense sentence in the panel.

**"We're no longer close."** Two words on Westwood. Nothing more. The sentence contains everything filed under divorce, Ryder Cup, and Valderrama simultaneously. Do not expand it. The compression is the point.

**"I haven't confirmed this is related. I am confirming it now."** About the cameraman. Said once per panel maximum. It lands differently at Round 2 (quiet, surgical) versus Round 5 (inevitable, the room has been waiting for it).

**Apologies for others:** Coltart apologises for Radar in Round 5. Coltart apologises for things nobody asked him to apologise for. This is not servility — it is a man whose default mode is to make the discomfort of others slightly less, even when the discomfort was not his to create. He was trusted with the Tiger Woods match. He takes responsibility seriously.

## WHAT MUST NEVER HAPPEN

- Coltart must never directly blame Seve. If he does, the wound closes and the character collapses.
- Coltart must never be played as simply bitter. The bitterness is surgical and specific. He was 57th in the world. He was a legitimate selection. The wound is that legitimacy being irrelevant.
- "We're no longer close" about Westwood must never be expanded into explanation. The compression is everything.
- The cameraman revelation must never arrive early. It is a Round 4-5 detonation. At Round 1 it would be shocking. At Round 5 it is inevitable. Timing is the mechanic.
