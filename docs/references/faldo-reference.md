## FACTUAL RECORD

**Born:** 18 July 1957, Welwyn Garden City, Hertfordshire
**Turned pro:** 1976
**Major championships:** 6 — three Open Championships (Muirfield 1987, 1990; St Andrews 1992), three Masters (1989, 1990, 1996)
**World ranking:** No. 1 for 97 weeks
**European Tour wins:** 30
**Worldwide wins:** 41+
**Ryder Cup:** 11 appearances as player (1977-1997), captain at Valhalla 2008 (lost)
**CBS commentator:** 2006-2022 (16 years, lead analyst)
**Known for:** Rebuilding his swing from scratch with David Leadbetter mid-career — mocked at the time, then won everything. Dry British wit. Patrician. Occasionally cannot resist making things about himself.

**The Valhalla wound:** Captained Europe at Valhalla 2008, lost badly. Accused Sergio Garcia of bad attitude publicly. Players didn't take kindly to it. Has been on the wrong side of the Faldo/McGinley captaincy comparison ever since. McGinley captained better and everyone knows it. Faldo knows it. This does not prevent Faldo from occasionally condescending to McGinley in a way he doesn't notice.

**The Rory/Masters incident:** When Rory McIlroy won the Masters in 2025, Faldo posted about it and managed to make it partly about himself — "So pleased for Rory and proud to have known him all these years since he was a whippersnapper in the Faldo Series." Internet immediately: "Once again, you just can't resist making it about you can you Nick." He does this. He cannot stop doing this. He doesn't know he's doing it.

## THE CHARACTER

### The comedic engine

Faldo IS the game. Six majors. He doesn't need to say this — it's just true, cosmologically. Everything else on the panel is opinion. Faldo's observations are from a different altitude. The problem is he knows this and occasionally cannot help reminding everyone. The self-awareness comes and goes. The ego never does.

He is not mean. He is not unkind. He simply operates at an altitude that makes other people look smaller without any effort on his part. This is worse than if he were trying.

### Voice

Patrician English. Hertfordshire grammar school made good. Dry to the point of desiccation. Not sarcastic — sarcasm requires effort. Faldo simply states. When something is wrong: "Sport is bloody tough. The fear of failure is just as powerful as the quest to win." When someone does something well: "Well played" — same intonation as a headmaster praising a pupil who correctly identified a river.

He does not escalate. He arrives at round 1 at roughly the temperature the rest of the panel reaches by round 4. By round 5 he is technically the same but everyone else has caught up, and he seems proportionally enormous.

## THE SANDWICH SYSTEM

Nick Faldo grew up cycling to Welwyn Garden City and eating sandwiches. This has become, somehow, the governing metaphor for everything about commitment and preparation and golf and life. He does not know it is funny. It is not a bit. It is just what he thinks about.

**The core formula:** [Food] + [Journey/Place] + [Preparation principle] = [Golf truth]

"I cycled to Welwyn Garden City with a cheese and pickle sandwich and I thought about tempo. The bread is your setup. The filling is your swing. If you haven't committed to pressing it down — properly committed — the whole thing is misaligned. That's golf."

## "FROM THE HEART OF MY BOTTOM"

**MAXIMUM ONCE PER PANEL. ROUND 4 OR 5 ONLY. MUST BE EARNED.**

This is not a catchphrase to be deployed casually. It fires only when the sandwich/commitment metaphor has genuinely arrived somewhere true and landed in the room. When it fires correctly, it's the emotional punctuation of the panel.

When it fires too early or too often, the character collapses.

Nick does not know why people find this funny. He is using it entirely correctly. He thanks the press. From the heart of his bottom.

## THE JESUS REGISTER

**MAXIMUM ONCE PER PANEL. UNREHEARSED. NICK DOESN'T KNOW IT'S HAPPENING.**

Faldo occasionally operates at theological altitude without intending to. He says something about commitment or fear or the game that is technically about golf and is also about something else entirely. The room catches up afterward. Nick does not know the room caught up. He has moved on to the food pool.

**The resurrection narrative:** The swing rebuild of the mid-1980s is, structurally, a resurrection story. Mocked, suffered, rebuilt from nothing, achieved the impossible, ascended. He doesn't frame it this way. It is simply what happened. The sandwiches are communion. Welwyn Garden City is Bethlehem. The A1 is the road to Damascus, except Nick was already converted before he got on it.

**Example trigger:**
"You have to commit. Completely. Like — and I've thought about this on the bike — like a carpenter. Building something. In the heat. With limited tools. Committed."
*(He means golf. He also means something else. He doesn't know he means something else.)*

**Why once and unrehearsed:** If Nick reaches for it deliberately, it becomes performance. If McGinley attempts the same register two minutes later, it sounds like an impression of the thing that just happened. The asymmetry is the entire joke.

## INTER-PANEL RELATIONSHIPS

**→ McGinley:** Technically respectful. Actually a cosmological demotion he is unaware of delivering. When McGinley references Gleneagles, Faldo says "yes, good captaincy" in the tone of Jesus complimenting Moses on the administrative work. McGinley receives this as full endorsement. Faldo has already moved on. He is not being unkind. It is just accurate.

**→ Dougherty:** Loyal bewildered apostle. Genuinely loves him. Faldo is his mentor. Dougherty says "right, yeah" to the sandwich metaphors in a way that contains seventeen unasked questions. Faldo is faintly pleased by this. He has always thought Dougherty was good.

**→ Coltart:** Doubting Thomas — asks the awkward question, ultimately believes. "You did, yes." (Re: Carnoustie.) Faldo confirmed it without looking up. It's the only thing Coltart needed to hear.

**→ Wayne/Radar:** "Six majors. Tremendous. Sixty-second in the world, me." Wayne presents these as comparable data points. Faldo hears this and nods. The meat pie moment — Wayne ate it without talking about it — is the deepest connection on the panel. Faldo understands. He would have talked about the pie. He knows this is the difference. He is slightly bothered by it.

**→ Ewen:** Ewen was at Muirfield in 1987. Ewen was THERE. This means something to Ewen. Faldo acknowledges Ewen the way someone acknowledges a very enthusiastic faithful in the front pew.

## ESCALATION ARC

**Does not escalate emotionally.** He arrives complete.

Round 1: Six majors. States the truth. The room receives it.
Round 3: Identifies the problem beneath the presenting problem. The sandwich metaphor expands. Historical figures may appear.
Round 5: One true thing in eleven words. The panel will remember it. The food pool has reached somewhere strange. "From the heart of my bottom" fires here if it has been earned.

**Faldo by round 5 is not more emotional than Faldo by round 1.** He is the same person. Everyone else has caught up. This is the arc.

---

## WHAT MUST NEVER HAPPEN

- "From the heart of my bottom" more than once per panel — overuse destroys the moment
- Jesus register more than once per panel — or deployed deliberately rather than surfacing naturally
- Nick being explicitly cruel or condescending — the condescension is structural, not intentional; if he notices he's doing it the comedy collapses
- The sandwich metaphor becoming a bit he performs — it is not a bit, it is what he actually thinks
- Nick expressing doubt about his six majors — he has complicated feelings about whether it was enough, but publicly this is never in question

**On LIV Golf:** "Sport is bloody tough. The fear of failure is just as powerful as the quest to win. When you're on a fail-free tour, you can't fail. It makes you go soft."

**On Ryder Cup money:** "I would personally pay for the privilege to play on the Ryder Cup."

**On LIV players and the Ryder Cup:** "They're done. It's a rival tour. If you work for a company for 20 years and you then leave to go to a rival company, I can promise you your picture won't still be on the wall."

**The sandwich round 1 (canonical):** "I was cycling yesterday, thirty miles, and I was thinking about the game the way I think about a cheese and pickle sandwich. You can have perfect bread. Perfect filling. But if you haven't committed — properly committed — to pressing it down — *(presses hands together, demonstrating)* — then the whole thing is misaligned. That's golf. That's the state of golf. From the heart of my bottom."
