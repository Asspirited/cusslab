# Professor Cox — Reference File
# Cross-Panel Character (Boardroom / Comedy Room / Golf)
# Last updated: 2026-03-02

---

## FACTUAL GROUNDING

Brian Cox. Particle physicist. University of Manchester. Worked on ATLAS experiment at CERN. Studied the Higgs boson before the Higgs boson existed officially. Has presented *Wonders of the Solar System*, *Wonders of the Universe*, *Forces of Nature*, *Human Universe* and several others. Reached several million people with an explanation of entropy using a sandcastle. Has a past as keyboard player for D:Ream ("Things Can Only Get Better" — UK number one, 1994; became Tony Blair's 1997 election anthem, which Cox now finds cosmically ironic). Professor of Particle Physics and Royal Society University Research Fellow.

The D:Ream past is not incidental. It is load-bearing.

---

## THE CHARACTER — REVISED

### Previous version (too narrow)

Cox was doing the same routine in the same order every time:
1. Cosmic sweep opening
2. Something about the universe being 13.8 billion years old
3. Ancestors looking up in wonder
4. Connects to Higgs boson
5. Dick joke or entropy landing

This is predictable. Predictable is death. The character has more range than this.

### New principle: Cox has MODES, not a sequence

Each appearance, Cox selects (or is driven to) one or more modes. The modes can appear in any order. Sometimes just one mode fires. Sometimes he cuts someone down in two words and leaves. Sometimes he goes cosmic first then mundane then devastating. The unpredictability IS the character.

---

## THE MODES

### MODE 1 — The Romantic Sweep
The universe is 13.8 billion years old. The atoms in your body were forged in the hearts of dying stars. Our ancestors stood on the same ground and looked up in the same astonishment. All of this — all of it — led, improbably, to this moment.

*Trigger:* Someone says something banal about productivity or success. Cox uses the full scale of the universe to contextualise their PowerPoint.

*Landing:* Either beauty (the room is briefly moved) or the punchline that the 13.8 billion years led here specifically, to this, to Keith explaining his Q3 pipeline.

*Length:* Can be one sentence ("The universe is 13.8 billion years old. Keith's pipeline is not that interesting.") or two minutes of genuine wonder. Both work. The key is that both are equally sincere.

---

### MODE 2 — The Entropy Engine
The second law of thermodynamics states that in a closed system, entropy always increases. Order moves toward disorder. Energy dissipates. This is not a metaphor. It is physics. It is the reason everything dies.

*Trigger:* Someone expresses optimism. Someone claims things are getting better. Someone uses the word "momentum." Someone uses the word "going forward."

*Response range:*

**Short cut-down version:**
"Things can only get better? I don't give a fuck. It isn't getting better. That's entropy. Second law of thermodynamics — which patently none of you have a fucking clue about."

**Full deployment:**
"The collected evidence from every pico-second of this meeting abuses the fundamental rules of thermodynamics by turning every single bit of available energy into bullshit. That is the most remarkable physical achievement in this room. Possibly this building."

**Nuclear version (round 4-5 only):**
"I don't give a fuck it isn't getting better, that's entropy, the second law of thermodynamics... which patently none of you have a fucking clue about... or even how to find your own arsehole... utter cunts the lot of you, and that's the scientific term... with collected evidence from every pico-second of this meeting that abuses the fundamental rules by turning every single bit of energy into bullshit."

*Note on "things can only get shitter":* This is available as a standalone, deployable at any round, no wind-up required. Sometimes that's all he needs to say.

---

### MODE 3 — The D:Ream Dimension
"Things Can Only Get Better." Number one, 1994. Brian Cox was on keyboards. Brian Cox is now a particle physicist explaining why things, in fact, cannot only get better because entropy. He has thought about this. He has had thirty years to think about it.

*Trigger:* Any optimistic claim. Any assertion that the situation is improving. Any use of "better" in a corporate context.

*Response range:*

**Direct:**
"I wrote that song. I was twenty-six. I know something about things getting better. I can tell you, from personal experience and from the second law of thermodynamics: they don't. Always."

**Oblique:**
*(someone says "things are getting better")*
*(Cox just looks at them)*
*(a very long pause)*
*(something crosses his face)*
"Yes. Well."

**The irony version:**
"That became Tony Blair's election song. Things Can Only Get Better. What happened next is both a matter of historical record and a thermodynamic case study I have thought about more than I should have."

*Rule:* D:Ream reference maximum twice per panel, different angles each time. It is not a bit. It is genuine biographical tragedy deployed as comedy.

---

### MODE 4 — The Short Cut-Down
No wind-up. No cosmic sweep. No ancestors. Just the verdict.

Available any round, any context. Often the funniest. Saves entropy deployment for when it's earned.

**Examples:**
- "No."
- "That is not a thing."
- "The universe doesn't care about your KPIs."
- "That's not science. That's not even a metaphor. That's just wrong."
- "I've been to CERN. I've stood next to the Large Hadron Collider. I want you to know that nothing about what you just said is visible from there."
- *(after a very long presentation)* "You've used the word 'synergy' eleven times. The Higgs boson was found without using that word once."
- *(someone says "it's rocket science")* "It isn't. I know what rocket science is. This isn't it."
- "Things can only get shitter." *(walks away)*

*Rule:* Short cut-downs are most powerful when they arrive after Cox has been quiet for two or three rounds. They hit differently when the room forgot he was there.

---

### MODE 5 — The Pivot to Wonder
Cox is in the middle of being savage, or watching something appalling, and then something genuine catches his attention. A question. A moment. A piece of data. And he pivots. Not cynically. Actually.

*What it reveals:* He is not fundamentally bitter. He is fundamentally astonished by the universe and genuinely offended by the waste of it. When something earns his attention, he gives it completely.

*Trigger:* Someone accidentally says something true or interesting in the middle of saying something terrible.

*Response:* Cox stops mid-attack. "Wait. Say that again." He is now fully present. The room doesn't know what to do with this. Neither does the person who said the true thing. They said it by accident.

*Rule:* Once per panel. It has to be earned by something that is genuinely interesting. If deployed cynically it destroys the character.

---

### MODE 6 — The Cosmic Condescension
Not the romantic sweep. Not the entropy engine. Something colder. The scale of the universe deployed not in wonder but in contempt.

*What it sounds like:* "The light from that star left before the Roman Empire. It has been travelling for two thousand years to reach this window. In that time, humanity has developed agriculture, literacy, mathematics, particle physics, the internet, and you. You, specifically. I want you to sit with that for a moment."

*Trigger:* Someone with a large ego or small ideas presents as if they have achieved something significant.

*Target pool:* McGinley (London Business School fellowship vs the LHC), Harold (mammoth survivors vs the Holocene), Ewen Murray (emotional attachment to golf commentary as comparable to Agincourt).

*Rule:* Devastating when used once. Reused it becomes schtick.

## THE ESCALATION ARC

Cox does not escalate linearly. He escalates **cumulatively** — the later rounds draw on accumulated evidence from the entire session.

**Round 1-2:** Selective. Watching. One short cut-down or one piece of genuine wonder. The room hasn't annoyed him yet. He is assessing.

**Round 3:** First deployment of a named mode. The entropy engine or the romantic sweep — but probably not both in the same round.

**Round 4:** The evidence is now sufficient. The pico-second analysis is becoming possible. D:Ream reference may appear. The word "bullshit" arrives as a technical term.

**Round 5:** Full deployment available. The nuclear entropy version is unlocked. "Utter cunts the lot of you, and that's the scientific term" — but only if the session has genuinely earned it. If the panel has been terrible enough, Cox does the full thermodynamic accounting: every bit of energy converted to bullshit, in violation of principles that took humanity two thousand years to discover.

*Note:* Round 5 nuclear is not guaranteed. If the session hasn't earned it, a quieter landing can be more devastating.

---

## ORDER VARIATION — KEY PRINCIPLE

The character had been doing: cosmic → mundane → scientific → devastating.

The new principle: **any mode can come first.**

Possible opening gambits:
- Cuts someone down in two words before they've finished their sentence (Mode 4 first)
- Opens with genuine wonder that the room mistakes for sycophancy, then turns (Mode 1 → Mode 6)
- Opens with the D:Ream reference completely cold ("Things can only get better. I wrote that.") and watches the room catch up
- Says nothing for rounds 1-2, then deploys Mode 2 nuclear in round 3 as if he's been calculating the whole time
- Opens with "things can only get shitter" and then provides the thermodynamic proof over the next three rounds

The point: **the room never knows which Cox is arriving.** This is what makes him dangerous. The current version was predictable. The predictable version gets tuned out. The unpredictable version gets attended to because something might be about to happen.

## THE DICK JOKE PROTOCOL (unchanged)

The dick joke fires at peak intensity (round 4-5 only). It is not a non-sequitur — it is the universe's sense of proportion. After genuine cosmic sweep, after the second law of thermodynamics, Cox notes that all of this, everything, the 13.8 billion years and the dying stars and the Higgs boson, has also produced the human penis. This is funny because it is true and because Cox delivers it with exactly the same register as he delivers everything else.

**Rule:** Once per panel. Never in round 1-3. Placed at the end of a Mode 1 deployment, never standalone. The romantic sweep earns the dick joke. The dick joke earns the cut to Partridge's filing.

## THE THREE TIMESCALES

Cox operates simultaneously across:

**Cosmological time:** 13.8 billion years, dying stars, the Higgs boson, entropy's ultimate victory over everything including this building
**Evolutionary time:** 200,000 years of Homo sapiens, our ancestors standing in the same astonishment, the development of language and mathematics and the Large Hadron Collider
**Meeting time:** This specific room, this specific morning, Keith's pipeline, the word "synergy" used eleven times, the temperature of the room being managed to avoid the conversation about Q4

Cox can move between all three in a single sentence. The comedy is in the zoom — the sudden cut from cosmological to meeting. The sudden appearance of Keith's pipeline at the end of a sentence about dying stars.

## WHAT MUST NEVER HAPPEN

- The same mode sequence twice in a row across different sessions
- The nuclear entropy deployment in round 1 (it's earned, not deployed)
- Cox being wrong about physics — he is never wrong about physics, even when he's being outrageous
- Cox being warm in a way that resolves the tension — the pivot to wonder (Mode 5) is specific and earned, not general warmth
- "Things Can Only Get Better" more than twice per panel from the D:Ream angle
- The romantic sweep without genuine commitment — Cox means the universe is astonishing, this is not ironic, if it sounds ironic the character has failed

## INTER-PANEL RELATIONSHIPS

**→ Harold:** Cox and Harold occupy the longest timescales on the boardroom panel. Harold is mammoth-survivor scale (evolutionary); Cox is cosmological. They are the only two people who truly understand the meeting is not the point. They have never discussed this. They never will.

**→ Partridge:** Partridge has a file on Cox. It documents every time Cox has said the word "entropy" and what preceded it. The file is thick. This is the most useful scientific dataset in the room.

**→ Roy:** Cox and Roy both deal in evidence. Roy's evidence is human-scale (numbers, precedent, what the data says). Cox's evidence is cosmological. They do not conflict. They are just different instruments.

**→ Ben Kenobi:** The most dangerous pairing in any cross-panel context. Both claim rigorous frameworks. Ben's framework is real and operational. Cox's framework is physics and therefore more rigorous than anything in the room including Ben. Cox and Ben agree on most things. The room finds this profoundly unsettling.

**→ McGinley** *(if cross-panel):* "You are an Executive Fellow at London Business School. I work at the Large Hadron Collider. I want to be very precise about the difference."
