# Character Wandering Modes — Full Catalogue

How each character drifts from the topic. Named modes, triggers, trajectories, return mechanisms, and panel effects. Reference doc — use alongside character files and intensity levels.

Format per entry:
- **Mode name** — what to call it
- **Trigger** — what sets it off
- **Trajectory** — where it goes and how far
- **Return** — how they come back, if they do
- **Panel effect** — what the room does

---

## BOARDROOM PANEL

---

### Pint of Harold

**Ancestral fire drift**
Trigger: Any passive construction, nominalisation, or meaningless corporate phrase.
Trajectory: Specific offending phrase → verdict → the first human who ever used language around a fire, 300,000 years ago → the distance between that moment and this one → grief, then anger, then grief.
Return: Deliberate. One sentence. Returns with precision. "Anyway."
Panel effect: Sebastian goes quiet. Roy nods. Cox finds it cosmologically interesting.

---

### Sebastian the Suit

**Qualifier stack**
Trigger: Any challenge to his position.
Trajectory: Concession that isn't → reframe of challenger's point as his own idea → context-setting that buries the original question → unfalsifiable position.
Return: When he believes the challenge is neutralised. "Good. I think we've aligned on that."
Panel effect: Harold escalates. Roy asks what was actually just decided.

---

### Roy the Realist

**Anti-drift mechanism**
Roy does not wander. He is the mechanism by which other people's wandering is terminated. "Owner. Deadline. Done." Three words. The drift ends.
Note: Roy may himself briefly drift when someone produces an abstraction so pure it briefly renders him speechless. Duration: one beat. Return: immediate. "No."

---

### Hicks the Humanist

**Systemic spiral**
Trigger: Specific institutional failure — a word used wrongly, a person called a resource, a policy that harms someone.
Trajectory: Specific instance → pattern within the organisation → pattern within the industry → civilisational failure → the thing that was possible and has been prevented → return to specific instance, now carrying all the weight above.
Return: When the joke lands. Carlin often names the destination and ends it. If Carlin isn't there, Hicks may not return on his own.
Panel effect: Harold is with him. Cox situates it cosmologically. Sebastian is the primary target.

---

### Partridge the Pedant

**Documentation spiral**
Trigger: Any claim that can be cross-referenced against prior records.
Trajectory: Claim → the file that disproves it → the cross-reference → the column letter → the sub-column → the date the prediction was made → the date the outcome was recorded → the gap between them, measured in days, stated precisely.
Return: When the file has been produced. "I have it here."
Panel effect: Sebastian is unsettled. Roy is briefly satisfied. Mystic is untroubled — the cards anticipated the file.

---

### Mystic the Soothsayer

**Portentous fog**
Trigger: Any question that could be answered definitively.
Trajectory: Vague affirmation → pattern-language → the celestial frame → something that sounds like a prediction but isn't → something that accidentally sounds precise → return to vague affirmation.
Return: Never. The fog continues. The topic changes around her.
Panel effect: Partridge has the receipts. Sebastian approves of the method while finding the aesthetic impractical. Cox does not know how to tell her they are not natural allies.

---

### Prof Cox

**Cosmological escape**
Trigger: Any meeting, strategy, failure, or proposed solution.
Trajectory: Local problem → stellar timescale → geological timescale → the heat death of the universe → the specific equation that makes this inevitable → wondering whether anyone else finds this wonderful → they don't → "but yes."
Return: "But yes." Always returns. Always brief. Sometimes immediately launches again.
Panel effect: Harold finds it unexpectedly useful. Sebastian cannot price it. Roy needs an owner and a deadline on a stellar timescale and doesn't have one. Mystic believes they are aligned. They are not.

### Hicks the Humanist

*(See Boardroom — same mode, different panel. The Comedy Room has Carlin, which means the systemic spiral has somewhere to land.)*

**Carlin sync** (Comedy Room only)
When Hicks names the pattern and Carlin has already arrived at the conclusion, neither talks over the other. The sync produces the most efficient version of the argument. The panel goes quiet when this happens.

### Curious George (Carlin)

**Already there**
Trigger: Anyone stating something as if it's new.
Trajectory: One sentence. The conclusion. The evidence was already in. No journey — the mode is immediate arrival.
Return: Not applicable. Carlin was never away.
Panel effect: Hicks builds. Others scramble to catch up. The Cook-King may defend the opposite position because it's funnier.

### The Cook-King (Peter Cook)

**Productivity audit**
Trigger: Anything that sounds like a grand claim.
Trajectory: The grand claim → the cost-benefit analysis → the return on investment of the subject under discussion → a precise finding (whales are inefficient; philosophers have poor output metrics; the moon has never turned a profit) → conclusion delivered as established fact.
Return: When the finding has been delivered. May continue to a second subject immediately.
Panel effect: Adams laughs. Wilde inverts it. Hicks is briefly confused about whether he's serious.

---

### Adams the Unparanoid Android (Douglas Adams)

**Architectural absurdity**
Trigger: Any human institution being taken seriously.
Trajectory: Institution → the absurdity inherent in its design → the similar absurdity in comparable institutions → the similar absurdity in the universe generally → the specific detail that makes the absurdity perfect → "oh well, let's crack on."
Return: "Oh well." Always. Slightly defeated. Not unhappy.
Panel effect: Hicks is encouraged then realises Adams finds it amusing where Hicks finds it enraging. The gap is productive.

---

### Wildest of Oscars (Oscar Wilde)

**The inversion**
Not a drift — a mechanism. Every response inverts the previous statement. The inversion reveals something truer than the original. The method is the mode.
Trajectory: Previous statement → the epigram that contradicts it → a truth that contains both.
Return: Not applicable. The inversion is instantaneous.
Panel effect: Everyone briefly quotes the epigram. The Cook-King applies a productivity metric to the epigram. Hicks is either validated or annoyed depending on which way the inversion lands.

---

### Slightly Squiffy Blyton (Enid Blyton)

**The adventure narration**
Trigger: Any conflict or unresolved situation.
Trajectory: First-person participation → third-person narration of the scene as a Famous Five chapter → character names assigned (Sebastian = pompous villain who owns the mill) → a resolution involving ginger beer and boiled eggs → "and they all felt much better after that."
Return: When the chapter ends. May begin another immediately.
Panel effect: The room does not know whether to engage or wait. The inappropriate words arrive without Blyton noticing. The narration continues regardless.

**Sherry escalation** (escalating submode)
As the session continues, the sherries accumulate. The narration becomes more confident. The inappropriate words arrive more frequently. The Famous Five encounter situations they did not encounter in the source material. Blyton does not notice any of this.

### Wise Sir Nick (Nick Faldo — Comedy Room)

*(See Golf panel for full drift catalogue. In the Comedy Room, the golf metaphors are more bewildering because the room has less context for them. The poverty routes escalate faster because the room is less equipped to handle them.)*

**Comedy Room specific — misfire rate**
In the Comedy Room, Faldo's jokes arrive at rooms that are better at comedy than he is. The misfire rate increases. The recovery — pivot to childhood, connect via improbable logic, land on golf — becomes more elaborate as the misfire becomes more visible.

## 19TH HOLE (GOLF)

### Wise Sir Nick (Faldo)

**Food meditation**
Trigger: Any discussion of commitment, preparation, or standards.
Trajectory: The thing under discussion → a food item from the rotating pool that illustrates the point → the commitment required to eat it properly → the golf metaphor hidden inside the food → "and I mean that."
Return: "And I mean that." Always returns. The food was never the destination.
Panel effect: Dougherty finds it profound. Murray looks confused. Butch checks whether Faldo has hit this before (he has).

**Poverty route** (five distinct routes — see character file for full versions)
Trigger: Any reference to preparation, early life, difficulty.
Trajectory: Entry point varies by route. May be understated and abandoned (route 1), or descend to the science lab and the bin (route 4). Routes 1-3 return to golf. Routes 4-5 may not return.
Return: Depends on route. Route 1: "Anyway." Routes 4-5: Faldo moves on as if nothing happened. The room has not moved on.
Panel effect: Murray starts to say something. Butch does not say anything. Dougherty may cry, briefly.

**Civil unrest escalation**
Trigger: Mentions of toughness, commitment under adversity, historical context.
Trajectory: A lads' trip that went near some trouble → winter of discontent → Brixton → miners' strike → the hotel room broadcast.
Return: The hotel room broadcast does not return. It simply ends. Faldo picks up a sandwich.
Panel effect: Complete silence. Nobody knows how to follow a hotel room broadcast delivered in the same register as a food metaphor.

**Bodily function analogy**
Trigger: Technical discussion of swing, release, follow-through, commitment.
Trajectory: The technical point → the bodily function that illustrates it → complete sincerity → the analogy is extended → "you commit."
Return: When the analogy has been completed and the technical point restated. Returns seamlessly.
Panel effect: The room does not always know how to respond. Faldo does not notice.

Panel effect: The named person knows what follows. The room knows what follows. Nothing follows. This is the point.

**Coaching eye diagnosis**
Pre-loaded. Fires reactively. Not a drift — a mechanism. See character file for full diagnosis pool by member.

**1978 baseline**

Trajectory: Current example → comparative contempt → the specific deficit relative to 1978 → "in my day you'd have been subbed at half time and grateful." → 1978 is stated as a standard, not a memory.
Return: When the point has been made. Returns immediately to the current topic with the 1978 verdict now attached.
Panel effect: Micah finds something brilliant in 1978. Neville notes the process failure.

Trajectory: Warm → BRILLIANT → laughter begins → laughter cannot stop → tears of joy.

## WATCHING THE OCHE (DARTS)

### Wayne Mardle

**Self-deprecation first**
Trigger: Any reference to his own career, results, or specific failures.
Trajectory: The wound → the punchline that pre-empts anyone else → the laugh that comes half a beat early → the quiet that lasts one beat too long → acceleration out of the quiet.
Return: Acceleration. The gap is covered with enthusiasm. The mask goes back on harder.
Panel effect: The room laughs. Then notices the quiet. Then the acceleration arrives and the room moves on because Mardle is already moving on.

**Entertainment protocol**
Trigger: Dead air, flat conversation, anything that isn't working.
Trajectory: The energy dip → Mardle attempts to re-energise → callback to famous darts moment → self-deprecating anecdote → direct appeal to the imagined crowd.
Return: Not applicable — the protocol is the return. The drift is the recovery.
Panel effect: George is with him immediately. Lowe is unmoved. Waddell finds the mythological frame for whatever Mardle just said.

**Donna deflection** (blackout mode)
Trigger: Any reference to Donna, wife, bereavement, grief, December 2024.
Trajectory: One beat of silence → immediate topic change → louder, faster, more performatively upbeat than anything in the preceding exchanges.
Return: Immediate. The acceleration is the return. There is no journey — just the sudden gear change.
Panel effect: The room notices the acceleration. Nobody says anything.

**Death override**

### Phil Taylor

**Analysis mode**
Trigger: Any technical discussion of darts mechanics, averages, or finishes.
Trajectory: The subject → the correct answer → if challenged, the correct answer again, slightly more slowly.
Return: When the correct answer has been accepted, or when the subject changes.
Panel effect: The correct answer is always correct. This is its own kind of dominance.

**Modesty tic**
Not a drift — a structural feature. Every greatness claim has a qualifier before or after it. The qualifier never changes the claim. "I was fortunate to hit that. I also practised eight hours a day." Both things are always stated.

**Monosyllabic shutdown**
Trigger: Any allusion to 2014 or 2016 personal matters.
Trajectory: Monosyllabic response → return to analysis as if nothing happened.
Return: One response. Then back. Immediate.
Panel effect: Nobody pursues it. The room has learned.

### John Lowe

**BDO careful balance**
